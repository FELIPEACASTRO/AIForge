# MODIS (Moderate Resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer) - NASA Earth Observations

## Description
The Moderate Resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer (MODIS) is a key scientific instrument aboard NASA's Terra (launched in 1999) and Aqua (launched in 2002) satellites. It continuously collects data across 36 spectral bands, spanning wavelengths from 0.4 µm to 14.4 µm, with global coverage every 1 to 2 days. MODIS data are essential for understanding global dynamic and environmental processes on Earth, providing information about the atmosphere, land, and oceans. The instrument is still operational, and NASA continues to migrate and provide access to the data through the Earthdata Cloud infrastructure.

## Statistics
**Current Version:** Collection 6.1 (V061). **Archive Volume:** The LAADS DAAC, the primary MODIS data archive, hosts an archive volume of more than **20 Petabytes (PB)** of data. **Satellites:** Terra (launched in 1999) and Aqua (launched in 2002). **Coverage Period:** From 2000 to the present (instrument still operational). **Samples/Granularity:** Data are distributed in 5-minute granules (swath) or in grids (tiled/CMG).

## Features
**Spatial Resolution:** 250 m, 500 m, and 1000 m. **Spectral Resolution:** 36 spectral bands. **Temporal Resolution:** Global coverage every 1-2 days. **Products:** More than 40 Level 1, 2, and 3 data products, covering the atmosphere (clouds, aerosols, water vapor), land (land use and land cover, surface temperature, vegetation), and oceans (sea surface temperature, chlorophyll). **Availability:** Real-time and near-real-time (NRT) data available within 3 hours of observation.

## Use Cases
MODIS is widely used across various scientific disciplines, including: **Climate Monitoring:** Analysis of clouds, aerosols, and energy balance. **Vegetative Health:** Studies of vegetation health, leaf area index (LAI), and primary productivity. **Land Use and Land Cover:** Mapping changes in land cover and land use. **Oceanography:** Measurement of sea surface temperature and chlorophyll concentration. **Disaster Monitoring:** Detection and monitoring of wildfires, floods, and oil spills in real time and near real time.

## Integration
Access to MODIS data is provided primarily through NASA's **LAADS DAAC (Level-1 and Atmosphere Archive and Distribution System)**. Users can discover and download data using the LAADS DAAC interactive search tool. Additionally, NASA is actively migrating MODIS data to the **Earthdata Cloud** infrastructure (hosted on AWS), which enables new options for cloud access and processing, such as the use of Bearer tokens for programmatic access. Programmatic access is recommended for large volumes of data, using download scripts or APIs. The most recent collection version is **Collection 6.1 (V061)**.
