# World Development Indicators (WDI) - Economic, Social, and Environmental Indicators

## Description
The **World Development Indicators (WDI)** is the World Bank's primary collection of global development data. It presents a comprehensive and up-to-date view of more than **1,500 indicators** of economic, social, and environmental development, covering more than **200 countries and territories** and spanning several decades (since 1960). The WDI is an essential source for policy formulation, research, and analysis of global trends, providing high-quality, internationally comparable annual data. The last metadata update was on **October 9, 2025**, ensuring the relevance and timeliness of the information.

## Statistics
**Size:** The complete CSV file (ZIP) is approximately **269.5 MB**. **Samples/Indicators:** More than **1,500 development indicators**. **Coverage:** More than **200 countries and territories**. **Most Recent Version:** **123** (updated on **October 9, 2025**). **Coverage Period:** 1960 to 2024 (Annual). **Usage Statistics:** More than 1.4 million views and more than 114 thousand downloads.

## Features
More than 1,500 development indicators. Coverage of more than 200 countries and territories. Comprehensive data across the economic, social, and environmental areas. Long-term time series (since 1960). Availability in multiple formats (ZIP, Excel, API). **Public** access classification with a **Creative Commons Attribution 4.0** license.

## Use Cases
**Economic Modeling:** Creation of predictive models for GDP, inflation, and other macroeconomic variables. **Public Policy Analysis:** Assessment of the impact of social and environmental policies at the global and national level. **Academic Research:** Comparative studies of development between countries and regions. **Sustainability Reporting:** Monitoring of indicators related to the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

## Integration
The WDI dataset can be accessed and downloaded in several ways:
1.  **Direct Download:** ZIP and Excel files are available on the data catalog page. The main ZIP file (CSV) is approximately **269.5 MB**.
2.  **API Service:** The World Bank offers an API service (DataBank) that allows programmatic access to the data, ideal for integration into applications and real-time analysis. The developer documentation is available on the resource page.
3.  **DataBank:** The World Bank's DataBank platform allows querying, visualizing, and exporting subsets of data.
