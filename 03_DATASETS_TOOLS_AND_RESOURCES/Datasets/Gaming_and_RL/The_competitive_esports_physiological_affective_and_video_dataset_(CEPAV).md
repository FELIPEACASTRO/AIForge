# The competitive esports physiological, affective, and video dataset (CEPAV)

## Description
The **Competitive Esports Physiological, Affective, and Video Dataset (CEPAV)** is a comprehensive multimodal resource, published in 2025, designed for researchers and analysts seeking to understand the complex interaction of physiological, affective, and behavioral factors in esports contexts. The dataset was collected from a study of 300 male Counter-Strike: Global Offensive (CS:GO) players with the goal of optimizing affect during esports tournaments. It includes physiological data, self-reported affective data, and video recordings of gaming sessions.

## Statistics
- **Participants:** 300 CS:GO players.
- **Recorded Matches:** More than 3,000 CS:GO matches.
- **Laboratory Gaming Sessions:** 552 sessions.
- **Dataset Size:** 820 GB of video data and 366 GB of physiological/behavioral data (totaling 1.186 TB).
- **Version:** Published in 2025 (Scientific Data, Nature).

## Features
- **Multimodality:** Combines physiological, self-reported affective, and video data.
- **Physiological Focus:** Cardiovascular responses (ECG, SBP, DBP, HR) and accelerometry data before, during, and after matches.
- **Affective Focus:** Self-reported affective data, including natural-language descriptions of in-game affective situations.
- **Behavioral Data:** Gameplay records and performance data.
- **Sample:** 300 male CS:GO players, aged between 18 and 32 years.
- **Structure:** Data collected across two laboratory sessions, daily gaming logs, and a one-month follow-up.

## Use Cases
- **Sport Psychology:** Investigating the relationship between affective state, physiology, and performance in esports.
- **Affective Science:** Developing and validating models for emotion detection and recognition in gaming contexts.
- **AI Training Systems:** Building AI-based coaching platforms that provide real-time feedback on the player's emotional and physiological state.
- **Multimodality Research:** Studying the synchronization and integration of different data types (physiological, behavioral, visual) for performance analysis.

## Integration
The CEPAV dataset is publicly available in the Open Science Framework (OSF) repository. Access is free and intended for research and non-commercial purposes. The data is distributed across three main components:
1.  **Physiological and Behavioral Data:** CSV files (366 GB) containing signals such as ECG, SBP, DBP, HR, and markers, with Python and R scripts for synchronization and processing available on GitHub.
2.  **Video Data:** Full HD recordings (820 GB) of 552 laboratory gaming sessions.
3.  **Questionnaire and Self-Report Data:** "CEPAV_data" spreadsheet with questionnaires, open-text data, and demographic information.
The code for processing and analysis is available on the project's GitHub.
