# Education Statistics (EdStats) - World Bank

## Description
The World Bank's **Education Statistics (EdStats)** is a vast and comprehensive dataset that offers more than 4,000 internationally comparable indicators on the education sector across 222 economies. The dataset covers the education cycle from pre-primary through higher education, including detailed metrics on access, progression, completion, literacy, teacher training, population, and expenditure. EdStats also incorporates learning outcome data from international assessments (such as PISA, TIMSS, PIRLS), equity data derived from household surveys, and educational attainment projections through 2050. It is a primary source for global analysis and research on education policy.

## Statistics
**Indicators:** More than 4,000 indicators. **Temporal Coverage:** 1970 to 2023. **Geographic Coverage:** 222 economies. **Versions:** The portal is constantly being updated, but the main dataset (EdStats All Indicator Query) is the most stable version. The World Bank notes that some indicators may not be fully up to date due to an ongoing redesign of the portal.

## Features
More than 4,000 internationally comparable indicators. Coverage of 222 economies. Data disaggregated by education level (pre-primary, primary, secondary, vocational, and higher education). Includes learning outcome data from assessments such as PISA, TIMSS, and PIRLS. Contains equity data and educational attainment projections through 2050.

## Use Cases
**Predictive Modeling:** Forecasting dropout rates, academic success, and future educational infrastructure needs. **Policy Analysis:** Assessing the impact of education and investment policies across different countries and regions. **AI Research:** Training Machine Learning models to identify equity disparities and optimize the allocation of educational resources. **Comparative Studies:** Conducting international comparative analyses of education performance and access.

## Integration
The dataset can be accessed and downloaded directly through the World Bank's Data360 portal in formats such as **Wide format (CSV/Excel)** and **SDMX CSV**. Alternatively, programmatic access is possible through the World Bank's **Data360 API**, which allows the retrieval of data and metadata using endpoints such as `/data360/data` and `/data360/indicators`. Using the API is recommended for integration into analytics systems and AI models.
