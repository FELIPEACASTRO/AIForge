# TIMIT Acoustic-Phonetic Continuous Speech Corpus

## Description
The **TIMIT Acoustic-Phonetic Continuous Speech Corpus** is a fundamental, reference dataset in the field of speech recognition, resulting from a joint effort between the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), SRI International, and Texas Instruments (TI). Released in 1993, it was meticulously designed to provide speech data for acoustic-phonetic studies and for the development and evaluation of Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) systems. The corpus consists of approximately five hours of English speech, with time-aligned transcriptions, and is widely recognized for its quality and rigor in annotation.

## Statistics
The original dataset, released in 1993, contains approximately **five hours** of speech. It covers **630 speakers** (438 men and 192 women), representing eight major dialects of American English. Each speaker read ten phonetically rich sentences, totaling **6,300 sentences**. The audio data is provided in single-channel, 16-bit PCM format, with a sampling rate of **16 kHz**. The corpus is divided into test and training subsets, balanced for phonetic and dialectal coverage.

## Features
TIMIT's main features include orthographic, phonetic, and word transcriptions, all time-aligned and manually verified. The audio files are accompanied by detailed speaker metadata, such as gender, dialect, date of birth, height, race, and education level. Its detailed structure and annotations make it ideal for acoustic-phonetic analysis and for training ASR models that require phoneme-level precision.

## Use Cases
Although it is a classic dataset, TIMIT remains an essential benchmark for evaluating ASR models. Its modern use cases include: in-depth acoustic-phonetic studies; development and evaluation of new ASR algorithms; a basis for creating derived multimodal datasets, such as **TCD-TIMIT** (for audiovisual speech recognition); research in speech synthesis (e.g., TIMIT-TTS); and, more recently, in voice modeling and audio deepfake detection.

## Integration
The TIMIT dataset (LDC93S1) is distributed by the **Linguistic Data Consortium (LDC)**. Access is generally obtained through an LDC subscription, for standard members, or via purchase for non-members. The download is performed via Web Download, and it is necessary to accept the **LDC User Agreement for Non-Members**. Due to its historical importance, the dataset is also frequently made available on research platforms and open-source libraries, such as Hugging Face Datasets, although the official source for licensing and the most stable version is the LDC.
