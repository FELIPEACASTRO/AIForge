# SNLI (Stanford Natural Language Inference) Corpus

## Description
The SNLI Corpus (version 1.0) is a collection of 570 thousand English sentence pairs, written by humans and manually labeled for balanced classification with the labels **entailment**, **contradiction**, and **neutral**. The main goal is to serve as a *benchmark* for evaluating text representation systems, especially those induced by representation learning methods, and as a resource for the development of Natural Language Processing (NLP) models.

## Statistics
Dataset size: 20.4 MB (Parquet files). Sample count: 570,152 sentence pairs in total. Version: 1.0. Splits: Training (550,152), Validation (10,000), Test (10,000).

## Features
Natural Language Inference (NLI) or Recognizing Textual Entailment (RTE) task. Labels: Entailment, Contradiction, and Neutral. Sentence pairs (Premise and Hypothesis). Hypotheses generated by *crowdworkers* based on image captions (Flickr 30k and VisualGenome). High annotation quality with 98% agreement of 3/5 annotators on the validation set.

## Use Cases
Training and evaluation of NLP models for Natural Language Inference. Development of text representation systems. *Benchmark* for deep learning models on language understanding tasks. Basis for the creation of extended datasets, such as e-SNLI (with natural language explanations).
