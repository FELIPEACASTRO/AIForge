# Country And Region AI Source Coverage Matrix - 2026-07-07

This matrix starts the country and region layer for AIForge. It is not a leaderboard. It is a source-routing ledger for national AI strategy, AI governance, open data, compute policy, public-sector AI, and regional ecosystem evidence.

## Global Aggregators To Use First

| Source | Coverage role | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| [OECD.AI Policy Observatory](https://oecd.ai/) | Global AI policy, principles, indicators, and national initiatives. | Live source for AI policies and initiatives with national contributors and OECD expert updates. |
| [OECD.AI Policy Navigator](https://oecd.ai/en/dashboards/overview) | Country dashboards and policy initiative search. | Useful first stop before country-level official verification. |

## Expansion Protocol For All Countries

1. Start with the OECD national AI repository and UNESCO global ethics baseline.
2. Add official government strategy, ministry, regulator, safety institute, open-data portal, and national statistics links.
3. Add country-specific AI research hubs, public compute programs, national language data, public-sector AI projects, and AI procurement guidance.
4. Add private ecosystem sources only after official and academic sources are captured.
5. Mark each country as `official-core`, `aggregator-only`, `needs-native-language-source`, or `needs-verification`.

## Required Country Metadata

- Country/region, source language, official owner, publication date, last checked date, and policy status.
- Strategy, governance body, regulation, standards, AI safety, public-sector AI, open data, compute, skills, startups, and research coverage.
- Evidence level: official government, intergovernmental aggregator, academic/think tank, industry report, media, or community.
- Notes on translation risk, stale policy, federal/subnational split, and whether the source covers strategy, implementation, or evaluation.
