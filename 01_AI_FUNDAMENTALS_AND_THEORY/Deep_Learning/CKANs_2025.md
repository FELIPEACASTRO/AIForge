# Convolutional Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (CKANs)

## Overview

Convolutional Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (CKANs) are a groundbreaking convolutional neural network architecture that replaces traditional fixed-weight kernels with learnable, non-linear activation functions based on splines (from the KAN concept). This results in a significant improvement in parameter efficiency and expressive power, demonstrating competitive accuracy with up to 50% fewer parameters compared to classic CNNs. It is a fundamental advancement in CNN architecture.

## Key Features

- **Learnable Kernels:** Replaces fixed-weight kernels with learnable spline-based activation functions.
- **Parameter Efficiency:** Achieves competitive accuracy with up to 50% fewer parameters.
- **Expressive Power:** Enhanced expressive power due to non-linear, learnable kernels.

## Impact

CKANs represent a fundamental shift in CNN architecture, moving from fixed to learnable kernels. This innovation has the potential to significantly reduce the size and complexity of CNNs while maintaining or even improving performance, making them more efficient and accessible for a wide range of applications.
