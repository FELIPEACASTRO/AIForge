### Key Resources Integrated

**Models & Transfer Learning (25 resources):**

The search identified cutting-edge deep learning models and transfer learning techniques specifically designed for medical applications. These include specialized architectures for medical image diagnosis, pathology analysis, clinical decision support, and drug discovery.

Notable models include **Med-PaLM 2** (Google's medical LLM achieving expert-level performance on medical exams), **MedViT** (Medical Vision Transformer for multi-modal medical imaging), **nnU-Net** (self-configuring method for medical image segmentation), and **ATOMMIC** (Advanced Toolbox for Multitask Medical Imaging Consistency). Transfer learning approaches such as **RadImageNet** (pre-trained models for radiology), **Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT)** for medical foundation models, and **Domain Adaptation** for cross-hospital medical data were also documented.

Advanced techniques like **Few-Shot Learning** for rare diseases, **Self-Supervised Learning** for unlabeled medical data (Snap-MAE), **Continual Learning** for evolving medical knowledge (CLMS), and **Knowledge Distillation** for edge medical devices were thoroughly researched and integrated.

**Datasets & Features (25 resources):**

The search uncovered a comprehensive collection of medical datasets spanning multiple modalities and specialties. These datasets are essential for training and evaluating AI models in radiology, pathology, genomics, clinical informatics, and drug discovery.

Key datasets include **MIMIC-III** and **MIMIC-IV** (ICU clinical data with 40,000+ patients), **ChestX-ray14** (112,120 chest X-rays with 14 disease labels), **CheXpert Plus** (multimodal radiology dataset), **BraTS-Africa** (brain tumor segmentation for African populations), **SLICE-3D** (3D skin lesion dataset from ISIC 2024), and **TCGA** (The Cancer Genome Atlas with multi-omics data). Additional datasets cover COVID-19 imaging, diabetic retinopathy, drug-drug interactions, electronic health records, medical question answering, and clinical trials.

Feature engineering resources include **PyRadiomics** (radiomics feature extraction), **CSpace** (medical concept embeddings), **PhenoBrain** (phenotypic feature extraction), **DrugBank** (pharmacological features), and **TITAN** (pathology image-text alignment).

**Prompts & Medical LLMs (10 resources):**

The search identified advanced prompt engineering techniques and medical LLMs designed for clinical reasoning, diagnosis, and patient care. These resources enable effective use of large language models in healthcare settings.

Key resources include **AutoMedPrompt** (automatic prompt optimization for medical tasks), **Models-Vote Prompting (MVP)** (ensemble prompting for medical diagnosis), **Chain-of-Thought (CoT) Prompting** for clinical reasoning, **Med-PaLM 2** (Google's medical LLM), **AMIE** (Articulate Medical Intelligence Explorer for diagnostic dialogues), **DDI-JUDGE** (LLM-enhanced drug-drug interaction prediction), and **TrialGPT** (clinical trial matching framework).

Prompt strategies for symptom-to-diagnosis mapping, treatment recommendations, medical report generation, and patient education were also documented, providing practitioners with practical tools for deploying LLMs in clinical workflows.
