### Key Resources Integrated

**Models & Transfer Learning (25 resources):**

The search identified cutting-edge deep learning models and transfer learning techniques specifically designed for agricultural applications. These include specialized architectures for crop yield prediction, plant disease detection, biomass estimation, and precision agriculture monitoring.

Notable models include **DeepBioFusion** for biomass estimation from satellite imagery, **AgriTransformer** for crop yield prediction using multi-modal data, and **Prithvi-EO-2.0**, the IBM-NASA geospatial foundation model for Earth observation. Transfer learning approaches such as **Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT)** and **LoRA** for agricultural foundation models were also documented, enabling efficient adaptation of large models to specific agricultural tasks.

Advanced techniques like **Few-Shot Learning** for rare crop diseases, **Domain Adaptation** for cross-region agricultural image analysis, and **Knowledge Distillation** for edge deployment in precision agriculture were thoroughly researched and integrated.

**Datasets (20 resources):**

The search uncovered a comprehensive collection of agricultural datasets spanning multiple modalities and applications. These datasets are essential for training and evaluating AI models in precision agriculture, biomass estimation, and crop monitoring.

Key datasets include **BioMassters**, a benchmark dataset for forest biomass estimation using multi-modal satellite time-series data, **PlantVillage** for plant disease classification, **Agriculture-Vision** for aerial farmland image analysis, and **DeepWeeds** for weed species recognition. Additional datasets cover soil moisture monitoring, weather and climate data, livestock tracking, and crop yield statistics from authoritative sources like FAO and USDA.

Specialized datasets for vegetation indices (**NDVI**, **EVI**), land use and land cover mapping (**MapBiomas**), and high-resolution crop type mapping (**CropSight-US**) were also documented, providing researchers with diverse data sources for agricultural AI development.

**Features (5 resources):**

The search identified critical feature engineering techniques and spectral indices used in agricultural remote sensing and precision agriculture. These features enable effective extraction of meaningful information from satellite imagery, drone data, and ground-based sensors.

Key resources include **Awesome Spectral Indices (ASI)** and **spyndex**, comprehensive libraries for calculating vegetation indices like NDVI, EVI, SAVI, and NDRE. Texture features based on **GLCM**, **LBP**, and **Gabor** filters for crop health assessment were documented, along with topographic features (elevation, slope, aspect) and climate-based feature engineering techniques for agricultural modeling.
