# Reasoning & Agentic AI — 2026 Radar

The defining theme of 2026: **agentic AI going mainstream**, powered by reasoning models and self-verification.

## Key Shifts (2026)

- **Agentic AI is the headline** — systems that take a high-level objective, decompose it into steps, and execute autonomously across many software environments are now the dominant product pattern.
- **Self-verification solves error build-up** — the biggest blocker to long multi-step agent workflows (compounding errors) is being addressed by internal feedback loops that let models check and correct their own work mid-run.
- **Reasoning models matured** — the o1 / DeepSeek-R1 lineage (trade latency for accuracy via long chains + verifiable rewards) is now standard across vendors; frontier models meet or exceed humans on PhD-level science (GPQA), competition math (AIME/IMO-class), and multimodal reasoning.

## Agentic Frameworks & Protocols (current)

- **MCP (Model Context Protocol)** — de-facto standard for tool/context integration — https://modelcontextprotocol.io/
- **LangGraph**, **OpenAI Agents SDK**, **Anthropic Claude Agent SDK**, **AutoGen**, **CrewAI**, **Pydantic AI**, **DSPy**, **smolagents**, **Letta (MemGPT)**
- **Nous Research Hermes Agent** — rapidly integrating new open models (e.g. GLM-5.2)

## Agent Benchmarks (watch these)

- **SWE-bench Verified / SWE-bench Pro** — real software engineering
- **TerminalBench 2.0** — CLI + sysadmin
- **TAU-Bench**, **OSWorld**, **WebArena / VisualWebArena**, **GAIA**, **ARC-AGI-2**

## AI for Mathematics (2026 frontier)

- **Automated Conjecture Resolution** — a reasoning agent searches for proofs while a second agent formalizes them in **Lean 4** for line-by-line machine checking; reported to resolve an open problem in commutative algebra with essentially no human involvement, plus new counterexamples in algebraic groups and p-adic Hodge theory.
- **AI Co-Mathematician (Google DeepMind)** — an interactive multi-agent workbench spanning ideation, literature search, computational exploration, and theorem proving, with session state.
