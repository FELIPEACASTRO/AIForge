# M&A Due Diligence Prompts

## Description
The **Mergers and Acquisitions (M&A) Due Diligence Prompts** technique refers to the use of advanced language models (LLMs) and Artificial Intelligence (AI) to accelerate, enhance, and deepen the process of investigating a target company. In a competitive market, where speed and accuracy are crucial, AI acts as a "metal detector" in a "haystack" of data, transforming DD from a manual and time-consuming process into an agile, data-driven one.

The main benefit is the **acceleration of deal speed** and the **increase in accuracy**. AI can review millions of documents, contracts, and financial records in hours, not weeks, identifying patterns, anomalies, and risk clauses (such as "change of control" or "liability caps") that could be overlooked in a manual review. The prompts are designed to extract actionable insights, test the investment thesis, and anticipate integration challenges, allowing M&A teams to focus on the strategic interpretation of the data rather than mere collection.

## Examples
```
**1. Contract Risk Analysis (Legal):**
```
Act as an M&A lawyer. Analyze the following set of customer contracts [Attach Contracts]. Extract and list all "Change of Control" and "Termination for Convenience" clauses. For each one, assess the risk of post-acquisition revenue loss on a scale of 1 to 5 and provide a mitigation recommendation.
```

**2. Market Risk Mapping (Strategic):**
```
Act as a buy-side strategist. Build a heat map of acquisition targets in the {Industry} sector, ranked by the likelihood of integration success. Use public risk signals (debt spikes, C-suite executive turnover, litigation headlines). Deliver the top 10 in a table with short justifications.
```

**3. Valuation Pressure Test (Financial):**
```
Interrogate the valuation narrative of {Target Company}. Build a scenario matrix showing how macroeconomic shocks (e.g., a 200 bps interest rate increase) and supply chain risks could reduce the valuation by 30%. Present the result in a 3x3 table.
```

**4. Hidden Pricing Power Identification (Commercial):**
```
Analyze the last 12 earnings call transcripts of {Target Company}. Extract overlooked clues about customer segmentation and pricing power. Recommend two quick pricing experiments worth more than an 8% margin increase after the deal closes.
```

**5. Red Flags Checklist (Operational/HR):**
```
List the 12 red flags that harm post-merger morale in technology-enabled services. Classify each as Ignore / Mitigate / Deal-Breaker.
```

**6. Earn-Out Gamification Blueprint (Financial/HR):**
```
Design an earn-out that aligns the seller's psychology with a two-year revenue target. Describe the basket of metrics, the safeguards, and a "double dragon" "kicker" if they reach {X} in {Y} months.
```

**7. Cultural Shock Analysis (HR/Integration):**
```
Merge the {Acquirer} (data-driven, remote) with the {Target} (founder-led, in-person). Identify the three main cultural clashes and create a 30-day plan to neutralize them.
```

**8. Integration Timeline Back-Casting (Technology/Operational):**
```
We need to integrate {Target Company}'s {System} into ours in 90 days. Back-cast the integration timeline, identifying the 5 most critical dependencies and the single point of failure.
```
```

## Best Practices
**1. Hybrid Approach (Human + AI):** Human expertise is **essential** to interpret complex findings, provide local and contextual knowledge, and exercise the final critical judgment. AI should be a triage and acceleration tool, not a substitute for the final decision.
**2. Focus on Interpretation:** Use AI to speed up document triage and anomaly identification. The time saved should be reinvested in the in-depth **interpretation** of the "signals" found, ensuring they are relevant and not false positives.
**3. Creation of Robust Hypotheses:** Use Large Language Models (LLMs) early in the process to create **robust hypotheses** about the Target and the market. This directs the Due Diligence (DD) toward the areas of greatest risk or value potential.
**4. Prompt Specificity:** Be as specific as possible. Define the AI's **role** (e.g., "Buy-side strategist", "IP lawyer"), the **output format** (e.g., "Table", "5-point summary"), and the **context** (e.g., "SaaS sector", "Earnings call transcripts").
**5. Continuous Pressure Testing:** Use prompts to test the Target's value narrative and integration plans. Create adverse scenarios (e.g., "Macroeconomic shocks", "Supply chain failure") to quantify the resilience of the business.

## Use Cases
**1. Accelerated Contract Review (Contract Diligence):** Bulk analysis of customer, supplier, and employee contracts to identify risk clauses (e.g., Change of Control, exclusivity, pending litigation).
**2. Financial and Accounting Analysis:** Rapid triage of financial statements, footnotes, and audit reports to identify anomalies, inconsistencies, or areas of accounting aggressiveness.
**3. Preparation of Request Lists:** Generation of highly customized and comprehensive Due Diligence (DD) document lists, tailored to the sector and transaction type.
**4. Legal and Regulatory Compliance Risk Assessment:** Scanning documents to ensure compliance with sector-specific regulations (e.g., GDPR, HIPAA, environmental regulations) and flagging gaps.
**5. Synergy and Cultural Shock Assessment:** Use of prompts to analyze internal documents (e.g., employee handbooks, internal communications) to anticipate cultural integration challenges and quantify the potential for cost and revenue synergies.
**6. Fraud and Vulnerability Detection:** Identification of unusual patterns in transactions or communications that may indicate fraud or cybersecurity vulnerabilities.

## Pitfalls
**1. Overreliance and Lack of Nuance:** Blindly trusting AI conclusions without human oversight. AI may struggle to interpret complex relationships, access critical local data, or capture subtleties in market conditions and corporate culture.
**2. Bias and Data Quality:** AI output is only as good as the input. If the Due Diligence (DD) documents are incomplete, disorganized, or contain biases, the AI will amplify these problems. Cleaning and curating the input data is crucial.
**3. Hallucinations and False Positives:** LLMs can "hallucinate" facts or create logical connections that do not exist in the source documents. It is mandatory that human professionals **verify the source** of each risk or anomaly flagged by the AI.
**4. Confidentiality Risk:** Using public or non-secure AI models to analyze confidential M&A data. It is essential to use private, enterprise AI solutions that guarantee data security and non-retention.
**5. Vague Prompts:** Using generic prompts like "Summarize the risks". This results in superficial outputs. The prompt must be highly specific, defining the role, objective, format, and output constraints.
