# Investment Research Prompts

## Description
Prompt Engineering for Investment Research is the art and science of crafting text inputs (prompts) that guide Large Language Models (LLMs) to perform complex tasks in financial analysis, asset valuation, and portfolio planning. It enables finance professionals and individual investors to extract valuable insights, conduct detailed analyses, and make data-driven decisions more quickly and efficiently. It is a crucial skill for leveraging AI in market analysis, risk management, and the identification of growth opportunities.

## Examples
```
1. **Market Trend Analysis:**
   ```
   #CONTEXT: You are an experienced financial analyst. Your task is to identify the current trends in the [Insert market type, e.g., technology stocks] market and explain how they might impact investment opportunities.
   #GOAL: Provide an analysis of the latest market trends and their potential effects on investments.
   #RESPONSE GUIDELINES: 1. Highlight 3-5 key trends. 2. Explain how these trends may affect investments positively or negatively. 3. Suggest potential opportunities or risks based on the analysis.
   ```
2. **Evaluation of Investment Opportunities:**
   ```
   #CONTEXT: You are a financial advisor. Your task is to evaluate the potential risks and rewards of a specific investment opportunity, such as [Insert investment type, e.g., shares of Company X].
   #GOAL: Provide a clear assessment of the investment, highlighting its pros, cons, and potential returns.
   #RESPONSE GUIDELINES: 1. Analyze the potential benefits. 2. Highlight the associated risks or challenges. 3. Provide a conclusion on whether it is a high-, medium-, or low-risk opportunity.
   ```
3. **Building a Diversified Portfolio:**
   ```
   #CONTEXT: You are an investment strategist. Your task is to design a diversified portfolio based on the user's risk tolerance [Low/Medium/High] and investment horizon [Short/Medium/Long term].
   #GOAL: Provide a portfolio that balances risk and potential returns across different asset classes.
   #RESPONSE GUIDELINES: 1. Suggest a mix of investments (e.g., stocks, bonds, ETFs, real estate). 2. Provide a percentage allocation for each asset class. 3. Explain the reasoning behind the allocation.
   ```
4. **Forecasting Future Growth:**
   ```
   #CONTEXT: You are a financial forecaster. Your task is to forecast the growth potential of a specific investment, such as [Insert investment type, e.g., the São Paulo real estate market], over a period of [Insert time frame, e.g., 5 years].
   #GOAL: Provide an analysis of the expected returns and growth based on data and market trends.
   #RESPONSE GUIDELINES: 1. Analyze historical data and current trends. 2. Forecast the potential growth or decline. 3. Suggest strategies to maximize returns or minimize risks.
   ```
5. **Financial Statement Analysis:**
   ```
   #CONTEXT: You are a forensic accountant. Your task is to analyze the financial statements of [Insert company name] and identify any red flags or areas of concern.
   #GOAL: Provide a summary of the key financial metrics (e.g., P/E, Debt/Equity) and an assessment of the company's financial health.
   #RESPONSE GUIDELINES: 1. Calculate and interpret 5 key financial metrics. 2. Identify abnormal trends or potential risks. 3. Provide a conclusion on the financial stability.
   ```
6. **Comparison of Asset Classes:**
   ```
   #CONTEXT: You are a financial advisor. Your task is to compare [Asset Class A, e.g., Gold] vs. [Asset Class B, e.g., Bitcoin] and provide insights into their benefits and risks.
   #GOAL: Help the user understand which asset class may be best suited for their goal of [Insert goal, e.g., inflation protection].
   #RESPONSE GUIDELINES: 1. Compare the pros and cons of each class. 2. Highlight potential risks and returns. 3. Recommend which one best aligns with the goal.
   ```
```

## Best Practices
* **Be Specific and Provide Context:** Include details such as the investment type, market, time horizon, and risk tolerance. Start the prompt by defining the AI's **role** (e.g., "Act as an experienced risk analyst").
* **Use Prompt Structure:** Use headers such as `#CONTEXT`, `#GOAL`, `#INFORMATION`, and `#RESPONSE GUIDELINES` to structure the request and ensure an organized, relevant output.
* **Ask for Reasoning and Sources:** Ask the AI to explain the reasoning behind its conclusions and, if possible, to cite the data or news sources used.
* **Iteration and Follow-up Questions:** If the initial response is not perfect, refine the prompt or ask follow-up questions to deepen the analysis (e.g., "Now, analyze the impact of the new regulation X on this stock").
* **Human Validation:** Always verify the information provided by the AI against current market data and reliable sources before making any investment decision.

## Use Cases
* **Fundamental Analysis:** Quick evaluation of companies' balance sheets, income statements, and cash flow.
* **Market Sentiment Analysis:** Summarizing news and social media to assess sentiment toward an asset or sector.
* **Scenario Modeling:** Simulating different economic scenarios (e.g., high inflation, recession) and their impact on a portfolio.
* **Generating Investment Ideas:** Identifying emerging trends, undervalued sectors, or assets that fit specific criteria (e.g., high dividend, low P/E).
* **Simplified Due Diligence:** Creating investment memos or institutional research summaries in minutes.
* **Financial Education:** Explaining complex concepts (e.g., options, futures, derivatives) in simple language.

## Pitfalls
* **Overreliance on AI Data:** LLMs do not have access to real-time or proprietary market data. The analysis is based on training data and public information.
* **Financial Hallucinations:** The AI may "hallucinate" facts, figures, or citations from sources that do not exist, leading to incorrect investment decisions.
* **Training Bias:** The model may reflect biases present in its training data, resulting in recommendations that favor certain assets or strategies.
* **Lack of Personal Context:** The AI does not know the user's full financial situation, tax obligations, or risk aversion unless explicitly provided.
* **Vague Prompts:** Generic prompts (e.g., "What should I buy?") result in equally generic and useless responses. Specificity is essential.
