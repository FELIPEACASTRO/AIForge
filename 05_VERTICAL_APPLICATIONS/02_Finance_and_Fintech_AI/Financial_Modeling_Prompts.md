# Financial Modeling Prompts

## Description
**Financial Modeling Prompts** refer to the art and science of creating effective instructions for large language models (LLMs) and Generative AIs, with the goal of automating, accelerating, and enhancing complex analysis and modeling tasks in the financial sector [1]. This technique is fundamental for finance professionals seeking to extract valuable *insights* from large volumes of structured and unstructured data (such as reports, meeting transcripts, and financial statements) [1]. Prompts are used for various categories of tasks, including performance summarization, scenario forecasting, key data extraction, *brainstorming* of financial strategies, report writing, and data restructuring [1] [2]. The use of well-crafted prompts allows AI to act as an assistant, performing complex calculations, sensitivity analyses, and even the creation of preliminary financial models (such as Discounted Cash Flow - DCF) based on assumptions provided by the user [2]. The accuracy and usefulness of the output depend directly on the clarity, context, and specificity of the instructions provided in the prompt.

## Examples
```
1. **Variance Analysis:** "Act as a senior financial analyst. Based on the Revenue, Expenses, and Net Income data from the last quarter (provided below), generate a variance analysis report comparing actual values with budgeted ones. Highlight the three largest variances and explain the possible causes. The output format should be an executive report in Markdown."
2. **Scenario Forecasting (Making Predictions):** "As a financial planning expert, forecast the impact on Gross Margin for the next quarter, assuming an 8% reduction in Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) and a 3% increase in sales volume. Present the result in a comparison table (Base Scenario vs. Optimistic Scenario)."
3. **Cash Flow Modeling:** "Create a 12-month cash flow projection model for a SaaS startup. Include the assumptions of revenue growth (2% per month), fixed and variable operating costs, and an initial investment of R$ 50,000 in Month 1. The result should be a table with Opening Balance, Inflows, Outflows, and Closing Balance."
4. **Valuation (DCF):** "Generate a simplified Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) model for a technology company with the following assumptions: Revenue Growth Rate (5 years): 15%, EBITDA Margin: 25%, Discount Rate (WACC): 10%, Perpetuity Growth Rate: 3%. List the steps and the estimated net present value (NPV)."
5. **Data Extraction:** "Analyze the text of Company X's Annual Report (10-K) (provided below). Identify and extract the following KPIs: Total Revenue, Earnings Per Share (EPS), and Net Debt. Present the data in JSON format with the keys 'KPI', 'Value', and 'Unit'."
6. **Investment Analysis:** "Evaluate the risk vs. return of a stock portfolio composed of 60% in technology and 40% in healthcare. Use the Sharpe Ratio as the main metric and suggest a risk mitigation strategy for the technology component."
7. **Data Restructuring:** "Take the transcript of the budget meeting (provided below) and reformat it into a list of action items and financial decisions made, using a bulleted list format (bullet points)."
8. **Sensitivity Analysis:** "Create a prompt to perform a sensitivity analysis on a profit projection model, varying the interest rate (from 5% to 10%) and the customer acquisition cost (CAC) (from R$ 100 to R$ 200). The goal is to identify the break-even point in each scenario."
```

## Best Practices
**Clarity and Specificity:** Always define the AI's role (e.g., "Act as a senior financial analyst") and be as specific as possible about the objective, output format, and data constraints. **Providing Context:** Include relevant data (history, assumptions, KPIs) directly in the prompt or through a data retrieval system (RAG). The quality of the output depends on the quality of the input. **Structure and Format:** Request the output in a structured format (table, JSON, Markdown) to facilitate integration and verification. **Human Validation:** Never blindly trust the outputs. Financial modeling is critical, and human validation of assumptions and results is mandatory. **Iteration:** Start with simple prompts and gradually add complexity and constraints (Chain-of-Thought, CoT) to refine the accuracy of the model.

## Use Cases
**Performance Analysis:** Quickly summarize quarterly or annual financial statements, highlighting KPIs and revenue *drivers*. **Forecasting and Budgeting:** Generate financial projections (*pro forma*), annual budgets, and sensitivity analyses for strategic planning. **Valuation:** Create valuation models (DCF, Comparable Multiples) for mergers and acquisitions (M&A) or fundraising. **Risk Management:** Perform scenario analyses and stress tests on portfolios or balance sheets to identify vulnerabilities. **Reporting and Communication:** Draft executive reports, variance analysis memos, and financial communications for the board or investors. **Due Diligence:** Extract and compare financial data from multiple acquisition targets from unstructured documents.

## Pitfalls
**Hallucinations and Inaccuracy:** LLMs can generate factually incorrect financial information or wrong mathematical calculations, which is dangerous in finance [3]. **Data Bias:** If the model is trained on biased data, the predictions and recommendations may reflect that bias, leading to suboptimal investment or capital allocation decisions [4]. **Lack of Specific Context:** AI does not have the company's internal context (culture, undocumented strategy, *soft information*), which can lead to incomplete or irrelevant models and analyses. **Regulatory Compliance:** The use of LLMs to generate financial reports or risk analyses may raise regulatory concerns (e.g., SEC, CVM) if the traceability and transparency of the process are not guaranteed [3]. **Confidential Data Leakage:** Entering proprietary and confidential financial data into AI platforms without proper security and privacy safeguards can result in the leakage of information.
