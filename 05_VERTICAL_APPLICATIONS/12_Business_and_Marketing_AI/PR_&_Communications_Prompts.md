# PR & Communications Prompts

## Description
Public Relations (PR) and Communications Prompts are structured instructions given to large language models (LLMs) to automate, optimize, and enhance tasks typical of the field. This includes generating press releases, crisis communication plans, speech drafts, social media content, sentiment analysis, and message personalization for different audiences. The technique aims to increase efficiency, ensure brand consistency, and free PR professionals to focus on high-level strategies and relationship management, turning the AI into a strategic communications assistant.

## Examples
```
**1. Press Release Generation:** "Act as a senior PR writer. Draft a press release about the launch of our new product, the 'Eco-Smart Watch'. The target audience is technology and sustainability journalists. The tone should be excited but professional. Include a quote from the CEO [CEO Name] and use AP Style format. Main focus: the 30-day battery and the use of 100% recycled materials."

**2. Crisis Communication Plan:** "Develop a 5-step crisis communication plan for the scenario in which a customer data breach is confirmed. The plan should include: 1) Key messages for the media, 2) Draft email for affected customers, 3) A 3-point script for the spokesperson, and 4) Social media monitoring strategy. The tone should be one of transparency and accountability."

**3. Speech Draft:** "Generate a 5-minute speech draft for the Marketing Director to deliver at the 'Future of Retail' conference. The theme is 'The Importance of the Omnichannel Experience'. The audience is composed of retail executives. The speech should be inspiring, include 3 market statistics, and end with a call-to-action to visit our booth."

**4. Social Media Content (Multi-Platform):** "Create a set of content for the announcement of a webinar event. Generate: 1) A long post for LinkedIn (professional), 2) A short and engaging caption for Instagram (visual and direct), and 3) 5 question-and-answer ideas for a Twitter Thread. The webinar theme is 'Cybersecurity for Small Businesses'."

**5. Sentiment Analysis and Response:** "Analyze the following customer comment on Twitter: '[Negative Comment]'. Classify the sentiment (Negative/Neutral/Positive) and then draft a 280-character response that is empathetic, acknowledges the problem, and directs the user to support via DM. Our company name is 'TechSolutions'."

**6. Pitch Personalization for a Journalist:** "Rewrite the following product pitch [Insert pitch] to make it more relevant to a journalist who specifically covers [Journalist's Coverage Topic, e.g., 'Fintech and Blockchain']. Highlight the aspects of the product that align with their interest and suggest an exclusive story angle."
```

## Best Practices
**Define the Role (Role-Playing):** Start the prompt by instructing the AI to act as a "Crisis Communication Specialist", "Press Release Writer", or "Social Media Strategist" to obtain an appropriate tone and format. **Provide Full Context:** Include essential information such as the target audience, communication objective, brand tone of voice, and specific details of the event or news. **Iteration and Refinement:** Use the AI's initial output as a draft and request specific refinements (e.g., "Make the title more eye-catching", "Reduce the text by 20%", "Add a call-to-action"). **Specify the Output Format:** Explicitly request the desired format (e.g., "Generate in a standard AP Style press release format", "List as 5 separate tweets").

## Use Cases
**Crisis Communication:** Rapid creation of key messages, FAQs, and response plans to manage reputation during negative events. **Media Relations:** Generating press release drafts, personalized pitches for journalists, and spokesperson biographies. **Social Media Content:** Developing content calendars, captions optimized for different platforms, and responses to customer comments. **Internal Communication:** Drafting corporate emails, memo drafts, and meeting scripts to ensure team clarity and alignment. **Speeches and Presentations:** Generating speech drafts, speaker notes, and executive summaries for events and conferences. **Sentiment Analysis:** Rapidly processing large volumes of public feedback to identify trends and concerns, informing PR strategy.

## Pitfalls
**Over-reliance:** Treating the AI's output as a final product without human review. The AI can get facts wrong, use generic language, or fail to capture cultural/legal nuances. **Lack of Context:** Vague or overly short prompts result in superficial and useless content. It is crucial to provide as much detail as possible about the audience, objective, and tone. **Bias and Ethics:** The AI can perpetuate biases present in the training data. It is the PR professional's responsibility to review the content to ensure it is ethical, inclusive, and free of discriminatory language. **Ignoring the Brand Voice:** Failing to include brand voice and tone guidelines in the prompt can lead to inconsistent communications that harm the corporate identity.
