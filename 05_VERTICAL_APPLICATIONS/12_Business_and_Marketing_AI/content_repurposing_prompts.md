# Content Repurposing Prompts

## Description
Content Repurposing Prompts are a prompt engineering technique that uses large language models (LLMs), such as ChatGPT, to transform a single, extensive piece of original content (for example, a blog article, webinar, e-book, or podcast) into multiple formats and distribution channels. The main goal is to maximize the reach, longevity, and Return on Investment (ROI) of existing content by efficiently adapting it for different platforms (social media, email, video) and target audiences [1] [2]. This technique automates tedious reformatting tasks and ensures that key points are retained while being presented in new and engaging ways [2]. The effectiveness of AI-powered content repurposing is high, with studies indicating that repurposed content can generate 2 to 5 times more engagement than newly created material [2].

## Examples
```
**1. Turn a Blog Post into an X (Twitter) Thread:**
`"Break this blog article about [Article Topic] into a series of 8 to 10 tweets that form a coherent and engaging thread. Keep each tweet concise, add a strong hook in the first tweet, and use a call to action in the last tweet for [Desired Action]."`

**2. Create a Short Video Script (Reels/TikTok):**
`"Based on this text [Paste the Original Content], create a 60-second video script for TikTok/Reels. The script should have 3 scenes, a visual hook in the first 3 seconds, and a voiceover that highlights the 3 most important points of the content. Include suggestions for on-screen text."`

**3. Generate a LinkedIn Post:**
`"Rewrite the executive summary of this report [Paste the Summary] into a professional LinkedIn post. The tone should be thought leadership, focused on [Target Audience, e.g., CMOs], and should end with a question to encourage discussion about [Specific Topic]."`

**4. Convert a Webinar into an Email Newsletter:**
`"Transform the transcript of this 45-minute webinar [Paste the Transcript] into a concise email newsletter. The newsletter should have a compelling title, a 3-paragraph summary of the main takeaways, and a clear call to action for [Link to the Full Content]."`

**5. Create an Instagram Carousel:**
`"Convert the 5 key points of this e-book [Paste the 5 Key Points] into a 10-slide carousel for Instagram. Create a compelling cover title, an introduction slide, 5 content slides (one for each key point), and 3 call-to-action slides (save, share, follow). The text should be short and impactful."`

**6. Generate Podcast Topics:**
`"Develop a detailed outline for a 20-minute podcast episode based on this article [Paste the Article]. Include an introduction, 3 main sections with 3 discussion points each, and 3 potential questions for a guest expert in [Area of Expertise]."`

**7. Create an Infographic (Outline):**
`"Create a detailed outline for an infographic that summarizes the data and statistics found in this study [Paste the Data]. Specify the exact text for the title, subtitles, and 5 data sections, ensuring that the visual hierarchy is clear."`

**8. Summarize for Different Proficiency Levels:**
`"Summarize this technical article about [Technical Topic] in three versions: 1) For a beginner (simple language), 2) For a manager (focused on business implications), and 3) For an expert (retaining the technical terminology)." `

**9. Create Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ):**
`"Analyze this complete guide about [Guide Topic] and generate a list of 10 frequently asked questions (FAQs) that a potential customer would ask, along with concise and direct answers based on the provided text."`

**10. Tone and Voice Adaptation:**
`"Rewrite this content excerpt [Paste the Excerpt] so that it aligns with our brand persona: [Describe the Persona: e.g., 'Fun and irreverent tone, but informative, like an experienced friend']."`
```

## Best Practices
**1. Content Audit:** Start by identifying your best-performing (evergreen) content using analytics tools (Google Analytics, SEMrush) to ensure you are repurposing your most valuable assets [2].
**2. Platform Adaptation:** Tailor the prompt and the content to the format and tone of each platform (LinkedIn, Instagram, YouTube). A generic prompt will result in wasted content [3].
**3. Mandatory Human Review:** Always review and edit the AI's output. Human intervention is crucial to ensure accuracy, maintain the brand voice, and avoid "WTF moments" (conceptual errors or inappropriate metaphors) [3].
**4. Inclusion of Brand Guidelines:** Provide the AI with detailed brand voice and tone guidelines, examples of approved content, and specific terminology to ensure consistency [3].
**5. Goal-Based Optimization:** Define clear conversion goals (not just traffic) for each repurposed piece of content and instruct the AI to optimize for those specific goals [3].

## Use Cases
**1. Content Marketing:** Transform a single blog article into a complete social media, email marketing, and video campaign, ensuring maximum exposure of the topic [2].
**2. Education and Training:** Convert technical manuals or long webinars into concise e-learning modules, FAQs, or executive summaries for different proficiency levels [2].
**3. Lead Generation:** Repurpose an e-book or case study into a series of blog posts and LinkedIn carousels to attract traffic and capture leads [2].
**4. SEO and Updates:** Use AI to optimize and update old (evergreen) content that is underperforming, transforming it into more engaging formats (such as infographics or videos) to improve engagement and ranking [2].
**5. Internal Communication:** Adapt complex quarterly reports into short internal announcements and Slack posts to ensure that all employees understand the key conclusions [2].

## Pitfalls
**1. Loss of Authenticity and Brand Voice:** Blindly relying on AI without providing detailed brand guidelines can result in generic content, diluting your brand's unique voice [3].
**2. Over-Optimization (Keyword Stuffing):** Focusing only on SEO prompts can lead to content that is "over-optimized" for search engines but of low quality and unengaging for human readers [3].
**3. Lack of Human Review:** Publishing AI output without review can lead to factual errors, inconsistencies, or inappropriate suggestions, resulting in "WTF moments" for the audience [3].
**4. "One-Size-Fits-All" Approach:** Using the same prompt and approach for all channels (blog, email, social) ignores the nuances of each platform, wasting the content's potential [3].
**5. Focus on Vanity Metrics:** Optimizing only for traffic or views, instead of clear conversion goals, can lead to ineffective repurposing that does not contribute to business objectives [3].
