# Customer Service Prompts

## Description
Customer Service Prompts are structured, specific instructions provided to Large Language Models (LLMs) or Artificial Intelligence (AI) systems to automate, enhance, and personalize customer support interactions. They transform generic queries into actionable tasks for the AI, allowing it to act as a first-line chatbot, an agent assistant (Agent-Assist), or a data analysis tool. The effectiveness of these prompts lies in their ability to provide context (customer history, company policies), define the AI's persona (empathetic, technical, solution-oriented), and specify the desired output format (summary, email, script). The strategic use of prompts allows companies to scale support, maintain brand consistency, and free up human agents to handle more complex issues that require empathy.

## Examples
```
**1. Empathetic Complaint Response Prompt**\n*   **Instruction:** \"Act as an empathetic and professional customer support agent. The customer [Customer Name] is dissatisfied with [Problem]. The company policy is [Relevant Policy]. Draft a response that acknowledges the frustration, apologizes for the inconvenience, and offers [Proposed Solution] in accordance with the policy. Keep a warm and solution-oriented tone.\"\n\n**2. Conversation Summary Prompt for Handoff**\n*   **Instruction:** \"Analyze the conversation transcript below between the customer and the chatbot. Generate a concise summary (maximum 3 sentences) that includes: 1) The customer's main problem, 2) The resolution steps already attempted, and 3) What the customer expects now. This summary will be used to transfer the case to a human agent.\"\n\n**3. Knowledge Base Article Creation Prompt**\n*   **Instruction:** \"Based on the 5 most frequently asked questions about [Topic/Product] over the last 7 days, create a Knowledge Base article. The article should have a clear title, an introduction, and 'Questions and Answers' sections for each of the 5 questions. The tone should be informative and direct. Use the persona of a product specialist.\"\n\n**4. Sentiment Analysis and Prioritization Prompt**\n*   **Instruction:** \"Analyze the following customer message: '[Customer Message]'. Classify the sentiment as 'Positive', 'Neutral', or 'Negative' and assign an urgency level from 1 to 5 (with 5 being the most urgent). Justify the classification and suggest the next action (e.g., 'Standard Response', 'Escalate to Manager', 'Forward to Sales').\"\n\n**5. Real-Time Response Suggestion Prompt (Agent-Assist)**\n*   **Instruction:** \"The customer just asked: '[Customer Question]'. The customer's context is: [Customer Context - e.g., Current plan, purchase history]. Suggest 3 concise and accurate response options for the human agent, based on the Knowledge Base and the customer's context. The options should be formatted as a numbered list.\"\n\n**6. Proactive Follow-up Email Generation Prompt**\n*   **Instruction:** \"Draft a proactive email for the customer [Customer Name] who purchased [Product] 30 days ago. The goal is to check satisfaction and offer a useful resource. The email should ask whether the customer is satisfied and include a link to [Useful Resource - e.g., Tutorial, advanced FAQ]. The tone should be friendly and supportive.\"\n\n**7. Upsell/Cross-sell Opportunity Identification Prompt**\n*   **Instruction:** \"Analyze the usage history of the customer [Customer Name]: [Usage Data]. The customer is using [Feature A] intensively but not [Feature B]. [Feature B] is an ideal complement. Suggest a concise message for the agent that highlights the value of [Feature B] and how it would solve the problem [Current Usage Problem].\"
```

## Best Practices
1. **Clear Path to a Human Agent:** Always provide an easy and visible route for the customer to escalate to a human agent, especially in complex or emotional cases.\n2. **Single Source of Truth (SSOT):** Train the AI only with a unified, verified, and up-to-date knowledge base to ensure consistent and accurate responses.\n3. **Transparency:** Be transparent about when the customer is interacting with the AI to build trust and manage expectations.\n4. **AI as a Co-pilot:** Position the AI as an assistance tool for human agents (Agent-Assist), not as a replacement, to increase productivity and reduce team resistance.\n5. **Continuous Monitoring:** Monitor technical metrics (precision, recall) and customer satisfaction metrics (CSAT, FCR) to continuously optimize the AI's performance.

## Use Cases
1. **First-Line Response Automation:** Chatbots and virtual assistants that answer 80% of frequently asked questions (FAQ) and routine queries (order status, business hours).\n2. **Real-Time Agent Assistance (Agent-Assist):** Suggesting responses, summarizing long conversations, and retrieving relevant information from the Knowledge Base for human agents during live interactions.\n3. **Sentiment Analysis and Prioritization:** Scanning incoming messages to detect frustration or urgency, automatically prioritizing high-risk tickets or VIP customers.\n4. **Proactive Support:** Using predictive analytics to identify customers at risk of churn or imminent problems and initiating a support contact before the customer needs to complain.\n5. **Personalization at Scale:** Adapting the tone, language, and product offers based on the individual customer's history and context.

## Pitfalls
1. **Infinite AI Loop:** Trapping the customer in a cycle of automated responses without the option to speak to a human, leading to frustration and brand damage.\n2. **Hallucinations and Inconsistencies:** The AI providing incorrect, outdated, or contradictory information due to poor training data or the lack of a Single Source of Truth (SSOT).\n3. **Lack of Empathy:** The AI failing to handle emotional or complex issues that require judgment and nuance, resulting in robotic and unsatisfactory responses.\n4. **Bias and Unfairness:** The AI inheriting biases from the training data, resulting in preferential or inconsistent treatment for different customer profiles.\n5. **Cost-Only Focus:** Implementing AI solely to reduce costs, ignoring the customer experience and collaboration with human agents.
