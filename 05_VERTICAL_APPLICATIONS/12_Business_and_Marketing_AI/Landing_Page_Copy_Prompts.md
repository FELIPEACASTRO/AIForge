# Landing Page Copy Prompts

## Description
The **Landing Page Copy Prompts** technique involves creating detailed instructions for large language models (LLMs) with the goal of generating high-conversion copy (copywriting) for landing pages. The prompt should be structured to give the AI full context, including the **offer** (product/service), the **persona** (target audience), the **conversion goal** (e.g., sign-up, purchase), the desired **copywriting structure** (e.g., AIDA, PAS), and the key **benefits/features**. This allows the AI to act as a copywriting assistant, accelerating the creation process, facilitating A/B testing, and ensuring the inclusion of essential persuasive elements such as magnetic headlines, social proof, and irresistible CTAs. The most effective process is collaborative: the AI generates volume and variety, and the marketing strategist refines the language and ensures alignment with the brand voice.

## Examples
```
1. **Full Structure Prompt (PAS):**
`Act as a senior copywriter. Create the complete copy for a landing page for a new online course called 'Master Productivity'.
- **Offer:** A 6-week course that teaches a system for saving 10 hours of work per week.
- **Audience (Persona):** Professionals aged 30-45, overwhelmed with tasks and emails, seeking focus and organization.
- **Conversion Goal:** Registration for a free webinar.
- **Structure:** Use the PAS formula (Problem, Agitation, Solution).
- **Key Elements:** Headline, Sub-headline, 3 paragraphs of Body Copy (Problem/Agitation/Solution), Benefits Section (3 items), final CTA.
- **Tone:** Empathetic, professional, and motivational.`

2. **Prompt for Headline and Sub-headline:**
`Generate 5 headline and sub-headline options for a landing page selling financial management software for small businesses. The headlines should focus on the benefit of 'eliminating the accounting headache'. The sub-headline should mention 'automating 90% of tasks'.`

3. **Prompt for Benefits Section:**
`Write the benefits section (Body Copy) for a free e-book on 'Content Marketing for Beginners'. Turn the following features into customer-focused benefits:
- **Feature 1:** Module on SEO. -> **Benefit:** 'Attract Organic Traffic Without Paying for Ads'.
- **Feature 2:** Editorial calendar templates. -> **Benefit:** 'Plan 3 Months of Content in Just 1 Hour'.
- **Feature 3:** List of free tools. -> **Benefit:** 'Save Hundreds of Dollars with the Best Free Tools'.`

4. **Prompt for CTA (Call to Action):**
`Create the copy for the final CTA section of a landing page selling a branding consulting service. The title should be 'Ready to Transform Your Brand?'. The supporting copy should use a scarcity trigger (only 5 slots per month). Generate 3 variations for the button text, in addition to 'Book Your Strategy Session'.`

5. **Social Proof/Testimonial Prompt:**
`Write an introductory paragraph for the testimonials section of a landing page. The copy should reinforce trust, highlight that the results are real, and use a transitional phrase such as 'See what our customers are saying about the transformation they achieved'.`

6. **Objection and Paradigm-Shift Prompt:**
`Identify and respond to the persona's main objection ('It's too expensive') for a monthly supplement subscription product. Create a short block of copy for the landing page that uses the 'cost comparison' technique (e.g., 'Costs less than a coffee a day') and reinforces the long-term value.`
```

## Best Practices
**Clarity and Full Context:** The prompt should be a complete marketing brief. Include the exact offer, the detailed persona (pains, desires, objections), the conversion goal, and the brand voice/tone. **Copywriting Structure:** Explicitly ask the AI to use proven copywriting formulas (AIDA, PAS, 4 Us) to structure the copy, ensuring a persuasive skeleton. **Focus on Benefits, Not Features:** Instruct the AI to turn product features into clear benefits for the customer. Use the "What it is" vs. "What it means for you" structure. **Iteration and A/B Testing:** Use the AI to generate multiple variations (e.g., 5 headlines, 3 CTAs) to facilitate A/B testing. Data analysis should always validate the generated copy. **Ethics and Transparency:** Ensure the prompt does not lead the AI to create exaggerated promises, false urgency, or misleading communication. Persuasion should be based on the clarity of the value offered.

## Use Cases
**Rapid Draft Creation:** Quickly generate the skeleton and initial content of a landing page, reducing creation time from days to hours. **A/B Testing of Elements:** Create multiple variations of headlines, CTAs, and benefits sections for conversion rate optimization (CRO) testing. **Overcoming Creative Block:** Generate new ideas and angles when the human copywriter is struggling. **Optimization of Specific Sections:** Focus on high-importance content blocks, such as the Social Proof section, the response to Objections, or the creation of a scarcity CTA. **Tone and Voice Adaptation:** Generate the same content in different tones (e.g., formal, playful, urgent) for different audience segments.

## Pitfalls
**Lack of Context:** Generating vague or generic prompts (e.g., 'Write a landing page') results in superficial and ineffective copy. The AI needs details about the offer and the persona. **Overconfidence:** Accepting the AI's output without human review. The generated copy may sound robotic, lose the brand voice, or contain factual inaccuracies. **Focus on Features:** Asking the AI to list product features instead of instructing it to focus on the benefits and the transformation the customer desires. **Ignoring Structure:** Not specifying a copywriting formula (AIDA, PAS) causes the AI to create disorganized and less persuasive copy. **Exaggerated Promises:** Using prompts that lead to hyperbole or unrealistic promises, which harms credibility and marketing ethics.
