# Brand Identity Prompts

## Description
**Brand Identity Prompts** are structured, detailed instructions provided to Artificial Intelligence (AI) models, such as Large Language Models (LLMs) or image generators, with the goal of defining, refining or generating core elements of a brand's identity. These prompts act as a **branding brief** for the AI, ensuring that the generated output (text, concept, image) is aligned with the company's mission, vision, values, voice, personality and strategic positioning. They are essential for maintaining the **coherence and consistency** of the brand across all touchpoints and marketing materials.

## Examples
```
1. **Personality Definition:** "Act as a branding strategist. My brand is [Brand Name], a [Business Type] in the [Industry]. Our core values are [Value 1], [Value 2] and [Value 3]. Describe our brand's personality using a brand archetype (e.g., The Sage, The Hero) and detail how this personality should influence our tone of voice and visual style."
2. **Unique Value Proposition (UVP):** "Create 5 Unique Value Propositions (UVP) for a [Service/Product] service that targets [Target Audience]. Our main differentiator is [Competitive Differentiator]. The UVP should be concise, benefit-focused and highlight our advantage over the competition."
3. **Tagline and Slogan:** "Based on our mission of [Brand Mission] and our personality of [Brand Personality], generate 10 tagline options. The options should be short, memorable and convey a feeling of [Desired Feeling, e.g., trust, innovation]."
4. **Voice Guidelines:** "Develop a tone of voice guide for our communication. Define the tone in three scenarios: 1) Crisis Communication, 2) Product Launch and 3) Social Media Interaction. For each scenario, list 5 keywords we should use and 5 we should avoid."
5. **Content Pillar:** "We are a [Industry] brand with the goal of educating [Target Audience] about [Main Topic]. Suggest 3 main content pillars that align with our mission and that can be turned into 10 blog/video title ideas for each pillar."
6. **Refining an Element:** "Our current mission statement is: '[Current Mission Statement]'. Rework it to be more inspiring, less technical and more focused on the impact we have on the life of the [Target Audience]."
7. **Visual Brief (for image generator):** "Create a visual moodboard for our brand's logo. The brand is [Brand Name], which sells [Product] and has the personality of [Archetype/Personality]. The style should be [Visual Style, e.g., minimalist, futuristic, organic]. Suggest a palette of 3 primary colors and describe the feeling the image should evoke."
8. **Brand Story:** "Write a 3-paragraph *Brand Story* that begins with the problem we solve for the [Target Audience], presents the company's founding moment and ends with our vision for the future. The tone should be [Tone of Voice]."
```

## Best Practices
1. **Provide Complete Context:** Include the brand's mission, values, target audience, market niche and existing differentiators in the initial prompt. The AI needs a complete "brief".
2. **Define the AI's Role:** Start the prompt by assigning a role to the AI, such as "You are a senior branding strategist" or "You are a copywriter specialized in luxury brands".
3. **Use Chained Prompts (Follow-up):** Don't expect the final answer in a single prompt. Use the first response as a base and refine it with follow-up prompts (e.g., "Refine this slogan to be more aspirational").
4. **Extreme Specificity:** Use clear placeholders (e.g., `[industry]`, `[target audience]`) and replace them with detailed information. The more specific, the less generic the output.
5. **Human Review:** The AI's output should be a starting point. Always review and adjust the content to ensure it captures the nuance and authenticity of the brand's human voice.

## Use Cases
*   **Brand Strategy:** Generation of mission, vision and values statements.
*   **Market Positioning:** Creation of unique value propositions (UVPs) and identification of competitive differentiators.
*   **Voice and Personality:** Definition of the ideal tone of voice (e.g., formal, fun, authoritative) and brand personality (e.g., brand archetype).
*   **Core Communication:** Development of slogans, taglines and *elevator pitches*.
*   **Content Marketing:** Structuring of content pillars and guidelines for creating on-brand materials.
*   **Visual Identity:** Generation of design concepts, *moodboards* and prompts for creating logos in visual AI tools.

## Pitfalls
1. **Generalization:** Using vague prompts (e.g., "Create a cool slogan") results in generic, useless content that does not stand out in the market.
2. **Loss of Authentic Voice:** Allowing the AI to write in a voice that is not the brand's, resulting in content that sounds robotic or inauthentic. The AI should amplify the voice, not replace it.
3. **Treating the AI Like Google:** Asking for broad definitions or information instead of requesting the creation of a specific branding artifact.
4. **Accepting the First Response:** The AI's first response is rarely the best. Iteration and refinement are crucial to branding success.
5. **Lack of Coherence:** Generating brand elements in isolation (e.g., a slogan, then a mission) without ensuring that all elements complement each other and align with the overall strategy.
