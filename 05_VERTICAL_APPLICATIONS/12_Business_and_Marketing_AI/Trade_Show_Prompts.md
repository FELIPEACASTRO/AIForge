# Trade Show Prompts

## Description
"Trade Show Prompts" is a category of prompt engineering focused on using language models and generative AI to optimize all phases of participation in trade shows, expos, and B2B or B2C events. This technique goes beyond the creation of generic marketing content, covering pre-event planning (target audience research, logistics), on-site execution (booth design, interaction scripts), and post-event follow-up (personalized emails, ROI analysis). Its effectiveness lies in the ability to provide the AI with the specific context of the trade show (name, audience, objectives, booth size, budget) in order to generate highly relevant and actionable outputs.

## Examples
```
1. **Booth Design (Generative AI):** "Create a prompt for Midjourney/DALL-E for a 6x3m booth for a B2B software company. The theme is 'The Future of Automation'. Use blue and white colors, with futuristic LED lighting and a central space for demonstrations. The backdrop should feature the slogan 'Automation Without Limits' in a modern, minimalist font."
2. **Lead Qualification:** "Act as a sales consultant. Create 5 lead qualification questions for the 'Tech Summit 2025' trade show, where our product is a CRM for small businesses. The questions should identify the company size, the main sales challenge, and the available budget."
3. **Follow-up Email (Hot Lead):** "Generate a follow-up email for a 'hot' lead who showed interest in our product X at trade show Y. Mention the specific conversation about the 'challenge Z' they face. The tone should be professional, direct, and include a CTA to schedule a 15-minute demo next week."
4. **Social Media Content (Pre-Event):** "Create 3 LinkedIn posts to promote our participation in the 'Feira Industrial Brasil'. The focus is the launch of the 'Alpha Robot'. Include relevant hashtags and a CTA to visit booth A12. The tone should be exciting and focused on innovation."
5. **Demonstration Script:** "Create a 2-minute script for the demonstration of the 'Inventory Management Software' product at the booth. The script should focus on the benefits of time savings and error reduction. Include an opening question and a closing line to transition into lead qualification."
6. **ROI Analysis:** "Based on the booth cost data (R$ 50,000), number of leads collected (300), and historical trade show conversion rate (5%), calculate the number of sales needed and the average contract value to reach break-even. Present the result in a table."
7. **Logistics Checklist:** "Generate a detailed checklist for the setup logistics of a 4x4m booth, starting 48 hours before the trade show opens. Include items such as 'Wi-Fi Test', 'Giveaway Delivery', 'TV Installation', and 'Team Briefing'."
```

## Best Practices
**Be Specific and Contextual:** Include the trade show name, the target audience, the main objective (e.g., lead generation, product launch), and the booth dimensions. **Use Examples (Few-Shot Prompting):** For follow-up emails, provide an example of a previous successful email so that the AI replicates the tone and style. **Define the Output Format:** Ask the AI to generate the result in a structured format (e.g., table, checklist, JSON) to facilitate implementation. **Focus on Visibility and Clarity (Design):** When generating prompts for booth design, specify the reading distance (e.g., "visible from 10 meters"), the brand color scheme, and the font (e.g., "sans-serif font, extra bold"). **Data Integration:** Use prompts to analyze data from previous events (e.g., "Analyze the 100 hottest leads from the last trade show and suggest 3 common pain points to personalize the follow-up").

## Use Cases
**Design of Exhibition Materials:** Generation of designs for banners, backdrops, flyers, and interactive displays. **Marketing Content Generation:** Creation of pre-event social media posts, product demonstration scripts, and content for booth screens. **Lead Generation Strategy:** Creation of lead qualification questions for the sales team and development of lead magnets specific to the trade show. **Follow-up Automation:** Generation of personalized email sequences for different lead segments (hot, warm, cold) collected at the event. **Logistics Planning:** Creation of detailed checklists for setup, teardown, and materials logistics.

## Pitfalls
**Generic Prompts:** Using prompts like "Create a follow-up email" without the context of the trade show or the lead will result in ineffective content. **Ignoring Physical Constraints:** Generating booth designs without considering the actual dimensions, lighting, or on-site visibility. **Lack of Personalization in Follow-up:** Sending the same follow-up email to all leads, regardless of the level of interest or the conversation at the booth. **Relying Solely on AI for Interaction:** Using AI scripts rigidly, without allowing the sales team to adapt the conversation in real time. **Not Defining the Tone of Voice:** The AI may generate content that is not aligned with the brand if the tone of voice (e.g., formal, fun, technical) is not specified.
