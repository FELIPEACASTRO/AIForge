# SEO Content Prompts

## Description
**SEO Content Prompts** are structured, detailed instructions provided to Large Language Models (LLMs) such as ChatGPT, Gemini, or Claude with the goal of generating or optimizing written content so that it has a high probability of ranking well in search engine results pages (SERPs). These prompts go beyond simple text generation, incorporating crucial SEO elements such as the strategic inclusion of keywords, the optimization of search intent, the creation of attractive titles and meta descriptions, the structuring of topics (outlines), and the identification of content gaps. They are essential tools for increasing the efficiency and scale of content production, ensuring that the generated material is relevant both to readers and to search algorithms.

## Examples
```
**1. Optimized Outline Generation:**
"Act as a Senior Content Strategist. Create a detailed outline (with H2s and H3s) for a blog article on 'Best Link Building Practices in 2025'. The main keyword is 'Link Building 2025'. The target audience is digital marketing professionals. Make sure the outline covers informational search intent and includes a section on 'AI Link Building Strategies'."

**2. Title & Meta Description Creation:**
"Based on the following article [INSERT ARTICLE DRAFT], generate 5 SEO Title options (max. 60 characters) and 5 Meta Description options (max. 160 characters). The Title and Meta Description must be persuasive and include the keyword 'Quick Vegan Recipes'."

**3. Content Gaps Identification:**
"Analyze the following article draft [INSERT DRAFT]. Identify 3 to 5 important topics or questions that the article does not address but which are crucial to covering the full search intent for the keyword 'Cybersecurity for Small Businesses'. Suggest a new H2 for each gap."

**4. Paragraph Optimization for Featured Snippet:**
"Rewrite the following paragraph [INSERT PARAGRAPH] to answer the question 'What is search intent in SEO?' concisely and directly. The goal is to optimize the paragraph for a Featured Snippet. Keep the answer to a maximum of 50 words."

**5. Keyword Clustering:**
"You are an SEO Analyst. I received the following list of keywords: [KEYWORD LIST]. Group them into thematic clusters (topics) and suggest a Pillar Content title for each cluster."

**6. Optimized CTA Suggestions:**
"Generate 3 Call-to-Actions (CTAs) for the end of an article on 'How to Choose the Best CRM Software'. The CTAs must be conversion-focused and direct the reader to 'Download our Free CRM Guide'."

**7. E-E-A-T Review:**
"Assess the tone and content of the following text [INSERT TEXT] and suggest how we can add more elements of 'Experience' and 'Authoritativeness' to meet Google's E-E-A-T guidelines. For example, suggest including case study data or expert quotes."
```

## Best Practices
**1. Be Specific and Contextualized:** Always include as much context as possible, such as the target audience persona, tone of voice, main keyword, secondary keywords (LSI), and search intent (informational, transactional, navigational). **2. Use the Expert Persona:** Ask the AI to act as an "SEO Specialist with 10 years of experience" or "Senior Content Strategist". This improves the quality and depth of the response. **3. Use Chain Prompting:** Break complex tasks (such as writing a complete article) into smaller, sequential steps (keyword research -> outline -> title and meta description -> section draft -> optimization). **4. Review and Optimize (Human-in-the-Loop):** Never publish AI-generated content directly. Review it to ensure factual accuracy, originality, brand voice, and compliance with Google's E-E-A-T guidelines (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness). **5. Optimize for Featured Snippets:** Include specific prompts to generate short, direct paragraphs that answer common questions, formatted to be used as Featured Snippets.

## Use Cases
**1. Optimization of Existing Content:** Improve the ranking of old articles by identifying content gaps, optimizing titles and meta descriptions, and rewriting sections for clarity and tone of voice. **2. Creation of Outlines and Briefs:** Quickly generate article structures (outlines) and detailed content briefs for writers, ensuring that all relevant SEO topics are covered. **3. Keyword Research and Clustering:** Assist in the brainstorming phase by suggesting long-tail keywords, LSI keywords, and grouping related terms into thematic clusters. **4. Generation of On-Page Optimization Elements:** Create titles, meta descriptions, header tags (H1, H2, etc.), and CTAs (Call-to-Actions) that are optimized for clicks and conversion. **5. Link Building Strategy Support:** Generate niche ideas for outreach, drafts of prospecting emails, and anchor text suggestions for backlinks. **6. Technical Optimization:** Assist in generating `robots.txt` rules or `.htaccess` rewrite rules for redirects, especially useful for content professionals with less technical experience. **7. Content Creation for Social Media and Videos:** Generate optimized YouTube descriptions and social media captions that complement the main content and drive traffic.

## Pitfalls
**1. Blind Trust in AI (Direct Publishing):** Publishing AI-generated content without human review. This leads to factual inaccuracies (hallucinations), repetitive content, and violation of Google's quality guidelines (E-E-A-T). **2. Keyword Stuffing:** Asking the AI to include the main keyword excessively, resulting in unnatural and penalizable text. **3. Ignoring Search Intent:** Generating content that does not match what the user is actually looking for when typing the keyword, resulting in high bounce rates and poor ranking. **4. Lack of Originality and Voice:** AI-generated content can be generic and lack a unique brand voice or original insights based on real experience. **5. Vague Prompts:** Using short, generic prompts (e.g., "Write about SEO") that result in superficial, low-quality responses, requiring significant rework. **6. Ignoring Data Limitations:** AI models may have a knowledge cutoff (data cutoff) and be unaware of the most recent developments in SEO or current events. **7. Content Cannibalization:** Generating new articles that compete with existing pages on the same site for the same keywords, diluting the authority of both.
