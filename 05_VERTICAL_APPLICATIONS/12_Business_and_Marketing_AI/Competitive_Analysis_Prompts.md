# Competitive Analysis Prompts

## Description
Competitive Analysis Prompts are structured and detailed instructions provided to Large Language Models (LLMs) such as ChatGPT, Gemini, or Perplexity, with the goal of performing competitive intelligence, benchmarking, and market analysis tasks. They turn the LLM into a business analyst or marketing strategist, enabling the rapid extraction of *insights* about competitors, market trends, positioning, communication strategies, pricing, and differentiators. Their effectiveness lies in the ability to provide context, define the AI's role, and specify the desired output format.

## Examples
```
1. **Detailed SWOT Analysis:** "Act as a business strategy consultant. Create a detailed SWOT analysis for [COMPETITOR COMPANY], focusing on its product [PRODUCT NAME]. Include 5 points in each quadrant (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats) and suggest a strategic action for our company based on this analysis." 2. **Pricing Benchmarking:** "You are a market analyst. Compare the pricing models (monthly, annual, included features) of the following companies in the [YOUR INDUSTRY] sector: [COMPETITOR 1], [COMPETITOR 2], and [COMPETITOR 3]. Present the data in a table and identify the most significant pricing differentiator of each one." 3. **Content Strategy:** "Act as an SEO specialist. Analyze the 10 most recent blog titles of [COMPETITOR'S BLOG] and identify the 3 main themes and the predominant tone of voice. Suggest 5 content topics our blog should cover to compete with them, while filling a gap they do not cover." 4. **Value Proposition Analysis:** "Based on the home pages of [COMPETITOR 1 URL] and [COMPETITOR 2 URL], extract and compare the Value Proposition of each. Which of them communicates most clearly with the target audience [YOUR TARGET AUDIENCE]? Justify your answer in 3 paragraphs." 5. **Identification of Differentiators:** "Act as a potential customer. What are the top 3 differentiators of [COMPETITOR PRODUCT] that make it attractive? What are the top 3 complaints or weaknesses mentioned in online reviews? Use this information to suggest a feature our product could add to outperform the competitor." 6. **Social Media Communication Analysis:** "You are a social media strategist. Analyze the communication style and the type of engagement that [COMPETITOR] is generating on [SOCIAL PLATFORM, e.g., LinkedIn]. Create a 4-point summary of what is working for them and 2 test suggestions for our own strategy." 7. **Competitive Intelligence Report:** "Generate a high-level competitive intelligence report about [COMPETITOR] for our executive team. The report should include: 1) Summary of Market Positioning, 2) Key Recent Moves (launches, partnerships), 3) Risk/Opportunity Assessment for our company. Use a formal and concise tone."
```

## Best Practices
- **Define the Role (Role-Playing):** Start the prompt by instructing the AI to assume a specific role (e.g., "Act as a Strategic Marketing Director" or "You are a Competitive Intelligence Analyst"). - **Provide Context and Data:** Include as much relevant data as possible (competitor names, URLs, sector, your own product/service). - **Specify the Output Format:** Ask for the result in a structured format (table, SWOT analysis, *bullet* list, report). - **Focus on the Objective:** Make the goal of the analysis clear (e.g., "Identify market gaps", "Compare pricing models"). - **Iteration:** Use the initial output as a basis for more specific follow-up prompts.

## Use Cases
- **SWOT Analysis of Competitors:** Rapid generation of Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats. - **Content Benchmarking:** Comparison of SEO strategies, most engaging titles, and content themes. - **Positioning and Messaging Analysis:** Evaluation of competitors' communication and tone of voice. - **Identification of Market Gaps:** Discovery of areas not served by the competition. - **Product/Service Comparison:** Detailed analysis of features, prices, and customer reviews.

## Pitfalls
- **Overreliance on Outdated Data:** LLMs may have outdated knowledge bases, requiring the user to provide recent data or URLs. - **Bias and Generalization:** The AI may over-generalize or introduce biases if the prompt is too vague. - **Lack of Depth:** Without specific input data, the analysis may be superficial and based on generic public information. - **Ignoring Human Verification:** The AI's output should always be checked and validated by a human analyst.
