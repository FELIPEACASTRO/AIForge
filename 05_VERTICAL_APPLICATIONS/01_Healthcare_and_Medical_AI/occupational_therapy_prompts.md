# Occupational Therapy Prompts

## Description
Prompt Engineering for Occupational Therapy (OT) is the application of structured commands in advanced language models (LLMs) to assist the therapist with planning, assessment, intervention, and documentation tasks. The AI acts as a clinical assistant, optimizing the professional's time in creating SMART goals, activity analysis, developing educational materials (such as social stories and relaxation scripts), and drafting reports. Ethical use is essential, requiring complete anonymization of patient data and human review of all generated material. The AI does not replace clinical judgment, but it enhances efficiency and creativity in practice.

## Examples
```
1. **Identifying Occupational Values:** "Act as an Occupational Therapy consultant focused on values. Create 5 powerful questions to help a client identify their 3 core occupational values (what is most important to them in their daily activities)."
2. **Activity Analysis:** "I am an Occupational Therapist. I need a detailed analysis of the activity of [activity, e.g., preparing a simple meal]. List the motor, sensory, cognitive, and psychosocial components required for successful execution."
3. **Creating SMART Goals:** "Create 3 SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) for a client with [condition, e.g., stroke with right hemiparesis] who wants to resume the activity of [activity, e.g., dressing independently]."
4. **Occupational Interview Guide:** "Formulate a semi-structured interview guide with 10 questions for an elderly client with mild cognitive decline, focusing on their occupational history, current routines, and satisfaction with occupational performance."
5. **Progress Report:** "Based on the following session notes [insert anonymized notes], write a draft progress report focused on [skill, e.g., fine motor coordination] to be sent to the physician. The tone should be professional and objective."
6. **Relaxation Script:** "Create a 5-minute guided script for a relaxation and mindfulness exercise focused on reducing muscle tension and sensory overload in a noisy work environment."
7. **Activity Adaptation:** "For a pediatric client with [condition, e.g., Sensory Processing Disorder] who needs to practice [skill, e.g., pincer grasp], suggest 3 creative adaptations for the activity of [activity, e.g., assembling a puzzle] that increase engagement and sensory tolerance."
8. **Creating Social Stories:** "Create a short, simple social story for a client with [condition, e.g., Autism Spectrum Disorder] about the routine of [routine, e.g., brushing teeth], including what to expect and the appropriate behavior."
9. **Educational Resources:** "Write an informative and accessible text (5th-grade reading level) about [topic, e.g., the importance of play in child development] to be used as educational material for parents."
10. **Occupational Therapeutic Metaphors:** "Create 3 simple, visual metaphors to explain the concept of 'occupational balance' (balancing work, rest, and leisure) for an adult client with burnout."
```

## Best Practices
**Complete Anonymization:** NEVER enter patient identifying data. Use hypothetical scenarios or 100% anonymized information. **Role Definition (Role-Playing):** Begin the prompt by defining the AI's role (e.g., "Act as an Occupational Therapy consultant specialized in pediatrics"). **Clinical Specificity:** Be as specific as possible about the condition, activity, and therapeutic objective. **Human Review:** Use the AI as a draft. The final product (report, goal, material) must be reviewed and validated by the Occupational Therapist.

## Use Cases
**Activity Analysis:** Breaking down complex activities into smaller components. **Goal Creation:** Generating drafts of SMART goals for treatment plans. **Material Development:** Creating social stories, relaxation scripts, and educational handouts. **Intervention Brainstorming:** Suggesting adaptations and environmental modifications for specific activities. **Documentation:** Drafting progress reports and session notes (with anonymized data).

## Pitfalls
**Overconfidence:** Believing that AI can replace clinical judgment. **Bias and Inaccuracy:** AI can generate inaccurate or biased information that does not apply to the real clinical context. **Breach of Confidentiality:** Entering confidential information, even by mistake. **Lack of Context:** Overly generic prompts lead to generic and useless responses. **Dependence:** Becoming dependent on AI for tasks that require fundamental clinical reasoning.
