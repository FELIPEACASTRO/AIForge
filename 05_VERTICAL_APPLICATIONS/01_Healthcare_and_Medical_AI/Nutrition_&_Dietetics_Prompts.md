# Nutrition & Dietetics Prompts

## Description
Artificial Intelligence (AI) in Nutrition and Dietetics refers to the use of language models and machine learning systems to assist both professionals (nutritionists, dietitians) and the general public in managing diet and health. The main focus is **personalization**, enabling the creation of meal plans, recipes, and dietary recommendations tailored to the user's individual needs, goals, restrictions, and preferences. AI shows potential for dietary tracking, personalized recommendations, and increased professional efficiency.

## Examples
```
1. **Detailed Meal Plan Creation:** `"Create a 7-day meal plan for a 35-year-old male patient, 85kg, sedentary, who wants to lose 5kg in one month. The plan should have approximately 1800 daily calories, be focused on the Mediterranean diet, include 5 meals per day, and exclude seafood. Provide the corresponding shopping list."` 2. **Generation of Specific Recipes:** `"Suggest 3 vegan and gluten-free dessert recipes, low in sugar, that use avocado as the main ingredient. For each recipe, include the preparation time and the approximate calorie count per serving."` 3. **Nutritional Analysis and Comparison:** `"Compare the nutritional profile of 100g of cooked quinoa versus 100g of cooked brown rice. Highlight the differences in protein, fiber, and micronutrient content (magnesium, iron). Which would be the better option for a vegetarian athlete?"` 4. **Outcome Simulation for Professionals:** `"Generate an outcome simulation for a meal plan focused on muscle mass gain. The patient is an amateur bodybuilder, 28 years old, 70kg, with a basal metabolic rate of 1700 kcal. The plan should have a caloric surplus of 500 kcal, and the macronutrient distribution should be 40% carbohydrates, 30% protein, and 30% fat."` 5. **Educational Content Creation:** `"Write a short, didactic text (maximum 5 paragraphs) about the importance of hydration for the elderly, explaining the risks of dehydration and providing 5 practical tips to increase water consumption, using accessible language."` 6. **Recipe Adjustment for Restriction:** `"I have a traditional carrot cake recipe. Rewrite the recipe so that it is suitable for a diabetic patient (replacing refined sugar with a natural sweetener) and lactose intolerant (replacing cow's milk with a plant-based beverage). Keep the flavor and texture as close as possible to the original."` 7. **Creation of an Optimized Shopping List:** `"Based on the 7-day meal plan you generated, organize the shopping list by supermarket section (Produce, Butcher, Dairy/Alternatives, Grocery) to optimize shopping time."`
```

## Best Practices
**Be Specific and Detailed:** The more information you provide (age, sex, weight, height, activity level, goals, restrictions, allergies, cultural/regional preferences), the more personalized and precise the result will be. **Define the AI's Role:** Start the prompt by defining the AI's role, such as "You are a sports nutritionist..." or "Act as a meal planning assistant...". **Use a Structured Format:** Ask for the output in a clear format (table, list, text with headings) to facilitate reading and application. **Professional Validation:** For clinical use, the AI's output should always be reviewed and validated by a qualified healthcare professional.

## Use Cases
**Personalized Meal Planning:** Creation of weekly or monthly menus for individuals or families. **Generation of Functional Recipes:** Adaptation of recipes to meet dietary restrictions (vegan, gluten-free, low FODMAP, etc.). **Nutritional Education:** Development of didactic content, blog articles, or social media posts on nutrition topics. **Clinical Support (for Nutritionists):** Simulation of plans, quick analysis of existing diets, and generation of shopping lists for patients. **Performance Optimization:** Creation of plans focused on specific goals such as mass gain, weight loss, or improvement of sports performance.

## Pitfalls
**Lack of Context:** Vague prompts lead to generic and ineffective plans. **Outdated or Incorrect Information:** AI may generate nutritional information or medical recommendations that are not the most recent or clinically correct. **Ignoring Individuality:** AI does not replace a complete clinical assessment, which considers factors such as medical history, blood tests, and emotional relationship with food. **"Half-Baked Ideas":** AI may suggest meal or recipe combinations that are nutritionally sound but impractical or in poor taste. **Bias and Lack of Ethics:** AI may perpetuate biases or, if poorly instructed, generate plans that promote eating disorders.
