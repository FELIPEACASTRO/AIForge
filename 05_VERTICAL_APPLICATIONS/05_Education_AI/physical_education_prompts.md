# Physical Education Prompts

## Description
Physical Education Prompts refer to the use of Generative Artificial Intelligence (GAI), such as ChatGPT and Gemini, by teachers and professionals in the field to optimize pedagogical and administrative tasks. The main focus is to enhance lesson planning, curriculum development, activity differentiation, assessment creation, and student behavior management. GAI acts as an assistant, accelerating content creation, from detailed lesson plans to adapting activities for different skill levels or cultural themes. Its effectiveness lies in formulating clear and parameterized prompts, such as the PROMPT method (Precise, Relevant, Objective, Measurable, Parameterized, Testable), ensuring that the AI's outputs are pedagogically sound and aligned with best practices in Physical Education.

## Examples
```
1. **Detailed Lesson Planning:**
`Act as a Physical Education curriculum specialist. Create a 50-minute lesson plan for 7th-grade students on the topic "Volleyball: Fundamentals of the Set and the Bump". The plan should include: learning objective (aligned with BNCC), warm-up, main practice task with differentiation for beginners and advanced students, an application game, and a closing with reflection. Consider a class of 30 students and limited resources (only balls and cones).`

2. **Adjusting and Differentiating Activities:**
`I have a basketball shooting activity for 5th grade, but I need to adapt it for a student with reduced mobility in a wheelchair. Provide 3 specific modifications to the game rules and equipment, ensuring the student can participate actively and successfully while maintaining the motor objective of the lesson.`

3. **Creating Assessment Criteria (Rubric):**
`Generate a formative assessment rubric for the "Rhythmic Gymnastics: Ribbon" unit for 9th grade. The rubric should have 4 proficiency levels (Beginner, Developing, Proficient, Exceeding) and focus on 3 main criteria: Technical Execution (use of the apparatus), Fluidity of Movement, and Body Expression. Include clear descriptions for each level.`

4. **Thematic and Culturally Relevant Activity:**
`Create a 10-minute warm-up activity for High School with the theme "Capoeira". The activity should be safe, involve basic movements (Ginga, Esquiva), and include a brief explanation of the cultural origin of Capoeira in Brazil, integrating history into physical practice.`

5. **Generating Mnemonics for Skills:**
`For the "Underhand Serve" skill in volleyball, create a 3- to 4-word mnemonic (in English) that helps 6th-grade students remember the key points of execution (foot position, contact with the ball, follow-through). Briefly explain the meaning of each word.`

6. **Behavior Management:**
`I am a Physical Education teacher and I need 5 positive reinforcement prompt phrases to use with 3rd-grade students who show persistence and effort during challenging motor activities, even when they do not achieve immediate success.`
```

## Best Practices
**The PROMPT Method for Lesson Creation (Trendowski, 2025):**
1.  **Precise:** Be specific and clear about what you want to achieve.
2.  **Relevant:** Ensure that the prompt is pertinent to the situation and the work at hand.
3.  **Objective:** Establish a clear goal or desired outcome for the chatbot.
4.  **Measurable:** Allow the effectiveness of the prompt to be evaluated.
5.  **Parameterized:** Include essential criteria to guide the response, such as grade level, objectives, total number of students, or other constraints.
6.  **Testable:** Design the prompt to be testable, allowing for the evaluation and improvement of performance.

**Other Best Practices:**
*   **Specific Approach:** Instead of asking for a complete lesson plan (holistic approach), ask for ideas for specific aspects of the lesson (specific approach), such as practice tasks or warm-ups.
*   **Critical Review:** Always review and adjust the AI-generated content to ensure it aligns with pedagogical best practices (e.g., 50% active time) and curricular objectives.
*   **Triangulation:** Use AI to generate ideas, but always validate them with other sources of information and your professional experience.
*   **Inclusion of Parameters:** Include the grade level, the number of students, the class duration, and the available resources to obtain more relevant results.

## Use Cases
*   **Curriculum Development:** Rapid creation of thematic units and instructional sequences for different grade levels.
*   **Lesson Planning:** Generation of lesson plans, learning objectives, and supporting materials (e.g., rule posters, equipment lists).
*   **Differentiation and Inclusion:** Adaptation of activities and instructions to meet the needs of students with different skill levels, special needs, or learning styles.
*   **Assessment:** Creation of rubrics, checklists, and multiple-choice questions to assess mastery of motor skills and theoretical concepts.
*   **Diversified Instruction:** Creation of activities and games with cultural themes or interdisciplinary connections (e.g., relating the physics of movement to sport).
*   **Behavior Management:** Generation of strategies, positive reinforcement phrases, and classroom management plans specific to the dynamic environment of Physical Education.

## Pitfalls
*   **Conflict with Best Practices:** AI may generate lesson plans that do not adhere to essential pedagogical principles of Physical Education, such as ensuring a high Motor Engagement Time (MET) (e.g., less than 50% active time).
*   **Lack of Repeatability:** The same request may generate different responses, requiring the teacher to continually refine the prompts.
*   **Inauthenticity and Generalization:** The generated activities may be generic, inauthentic, or decontextualized from the reality of the school, the students, or the available resources.
*   **Bias and Inaccuracy:** AI may aggregate incorrect or biased information, especially when requesting information about specific games or cultural practices, requiring verification.
*   **Overreliance:** Total dependence on AI can lead to a loss of creativity and of the teacher's ability to adapt in real time in the classroom.
