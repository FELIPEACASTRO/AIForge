# Special Education Prompts

## Description
**Special Education Prompts** are structured and detailed commands, provided to Large Language Models (LLMs) such as ChatGPT, to assist teachers and Specialized Educational Services (SES) professionals in creating materials, planning, and communication adapted to the needs of students with disabilities or learning difficulties. This *prompt engineering* technique aims to optimize the educator's time, offering support in curriculum differentiation, in developing Individualized Education Plans (IEPs), and in creating visual resources and social scripts. Its effectiveness lies in the professional's ability to provide specific context (diagnosis, skill level, pedagogical objective) so that the AI generates highly personalized and accessible outputs. Ethical use and human review are crucial to ensure accuracy and pedagogical appropriateness.

## Examples
```
1. **Lesson Plan Adaptation:** "Create a 45-minute lesson plan on the water cycle, adapted for 3rd-grade students with moderate cognitive disabilities. The plan should include the use of visual supports, a hands-on multisensory activity, and a connection to life skills (e.g., saving water)."
2. **Creating IEP Goals:** "Generate 3 measurable annual IEP goals for a 6th-grade student with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) who has difficulties with social communication. The goals should focus on initiating conversations and maintaining eye contact for 5 seconds."
3. **Social Script:** "Write a simple and positive social script for 'How to handle frustration during a difficult activity'. The script should have 5 steps, include suggested phrases for the student to use, and indicate a visual support (e.g., a picture of a breathing balloon)."
4. **Family Communication:** "Write a weekly progress note, in a friendly tone accessible to parents, about the progress of a 1st-grade student with Dyslexia in the skill of phonological awareness. Include a suggestion of 2 playful activities the family can do at home to reinforce the learning."
5. **Behavioral Intervention Suggestion:** "List 5 positive behavior support strategies that can be used for a 4th-grade student with ADHD who frequently gets up and interrupts the class. The strategies should focus on positive reinforcement and anticipation."
6. **Text Adaptation:** "Adapt the following paragraph about the French Revolution to a 2nd-grade reading level, using simple language and concrete vocabulary. [Insert the original paragraph]."
7. **Visual Material Creation:** "Create a step-by-step visual instruction list for 'How to wash your hands correctly' to be used on a routine board. Use short and simple sentences."
```

## Best Practices
**Be Specific and Contextualized:** Include as much detail as possible in the prompt, such as the student's disability (ASD, ADHD, Dyslexia, etc.), the grade level, the IEP (Individualized Education Plan) objective, and the desired output format (lesson plan, visual script, progress note).
**Use AI as a Draft:** Consider the AI's output as a starting point. Always review, adapt, and personalize the generated content based on your professional knowledge and the specific needs of the student.
**Focus on Accessibility:** Explicitly ask for visual resources, multisensory methods, simple language, and connections to life skills.
**Maintain Ethics and Privacy:** Never enter sensitive data or students' personal information in the prompts. Use AI to generate templates and structures, not to process confidential data.
**Explore Different Functions:** Use AI not only for planning, but also for family communication, behavior management, and creating visual materials (using AI image generators).

## Use Cases
**Differentiated Lesson Planning:** Creating lesson plans that address a wide range of skills and needs within an inclusive or SES classroom.
**Documentation Development:** Generating drafts of IEP (Individualized Education Plan) goals, parent-friendly progress notes, and assessment reports.
**Accessibility Resource Creation:** Developing social scripts, visual schedules, step-by-step instruction lists, and Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) materials.
**Family Communication:** Drafting messages, newsletters, and informational flyers for parents, ensuring clarity and a supportive tone.
**Behavior Management:** Suggesting positive behavior support (PBS) strategies and goals for Behavior Intervention Plans (BIP).
**Content Adaptation:** Simplifying complex texts to lower reading levels or adapting activities for different sensory modalities (multisensory).

## Pitfalls
**Over-reliance:** Blindly trusting the AI's output without proper professional review and adaptation, which can lead to inappropriate or inaccurate instructional materials.
**Ineffective Generalization:** Using generic prompts that do not include the student's specific context (diagnosis, level, IEP), resulting in outputs that are poorly personalized and ineffective for Special Education.
**Privacy Violation:** Entering personally identifiable information or students' sensitive data into the prompts, compromising confidentiality and professional ethics.
**Lack of Review:** Not verifying the factual accuracy or pedagogical appropriateness of the generated content, which is especially critical in areas such as IEP goals or behavioral interventions.
**Replacing the Professional:** Believing that AI can replace the clinical judgment, empathy, and human relationship essential in Specialized Educational Services (SES). AI is a tool, not a substitute.
