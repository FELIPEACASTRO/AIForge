# Plagiarism and AI-Generated Content Detection Prompts

## Description
Plagiarism and AI-Generated Content Detection Prompts are specific instructions provided to Large Language Models (LLMs) to assist in analyzing texts, identifying patterns that suggest traditional plagiarism (uncited copying) or generation by other AIs. Although LLMs do not replace specialized tools (such as Turnitin or Copyleaks), they can be used to: 1) **Style and Pattern Analysis**: Asking the LLM to identify passages with abrupt style changes, excessively formal vocabulary, or syntactic structures resembling typical AI outputs. 2) **Citation Verification**: Checking the compliance of references and citations with academic standards (such as ABNT or APA). 3) **Source Comparison**: Requesting a comparison of a text passage with a specific provided source, looking for similarities. It is crucial to understand that AI detection by LLMs is fallible and should be used as an initial indicator, not as definitive proof.

## Examples
```
1. **Style Analysis for AI Detection:** 'Analyze the following text. Identify and highlight any paragraphs that exhibit writing characteristics typical of Large Language Models (LLMs), such as excessively formal vocabulary, lack of authorial voice, or repetitive structures. Justify your analysis for each suspicious passage. [TEXT HERE]'

2. **Simple Plagiarism Check:** 'Compare the following text with the original source I will provide. Highlight all sentences or passages with more than 5 consecutive words that were copied without quotation marks or citation. [TEXT HERE] [ORIGINAL SOURCE HERE]'

3. **ABNT/APA Compliance Check:** 'Act as an academic reviewer. Verify whether all direct and indirect citations in the text are formatted correctly according to ABNT/APA standards. List the incorrect citations and suggest the correction. [TEXT HERE]'

4. **Identifying Excessive Paraphrasing:** 'Read the text and the original source. Identify passages where the paraphrase is so close to the source that it could be considered plagiarism of ideas or improper paraphrasing. [TEXT HERE] [ORIGINAL SOURCE HERE]'

5. **AI Detector Simulation:** 'Simulate being an AI content detector. Assign a score from 0 to 100% for the probability that the text was generated by an AI. Then, list 3 to 5 words or phrases that contributed most to this score. [TEXT HERE]'

6. **Rewriting for Originality (Ethical Use):** 'Rewrite the following paragraph in your own voice, keeping the meaning but changing the structure and vocabulary to ensure originality and avoid AI detection. [SUSPICIOUS PARAGRAPH HERE]'

7. **Coherence and Fact Analysis:** 'The text below deals with the topic [TOPIC]. Verify the logical coherence and factual accuracy of the arguments presented. Point out any 'hallucinations' or information that appears to have been generated without a basis in real data. [TEXT HERE]'

8. **Detection Refinement Prompt:** 'Based on your previous analysis, where you identified the passage [SUSPICIOUS PASSAGE], what would be the most effective prompt for a specialized plagiarism detector to find the original source of this content?'

9. **Character Manipulation Detection (Homoglyphs):** 'Analyze the text for homoglyph characters (letters from different alphabets that resemble Latin letters) that may have been inserted to try to evade plagiarism detection. If you find any, highlight them. [TEXT HERE]'

10. **Comparative Analysis Prompt for Two Texts:** 'Act as a forensic linguistics expert. Analyze Text A and Text B. Were they written by the same person, or is one a copy/paraphrase of the other? Justify your conclusion based on metrics of style, complexity, and vocabulary. [TEXT A] [TEXT B]'
```

## Best Practices
1. **Provide Context and Role:** Start the prompt by defining the LLM's role (e.g., 'Act as a rigorous academic reviewer') and the objective (e.g., 'Identify AI writing patterns').
2. **Use Limited Text Samples:** LLMs have context limits. For long texts, divide them into sections and analyze them separately.
3. **Request Justification (Chain-of-Thought):** Ask the LLM to explain *why* a passage is suspicious (e.g., 'Justify your analysis for each suspicious passage'). This increases transparency and reliability.
4. **Combine with Specialized Tools:** Use the prompt as an initial filter. If the LLM flags suspicions, use dedicated plagiarism and AI detection tools for the final verification.
5. **Be Specific About Standards:** If the focus is academic, specify the citation standards (ABNT, APA, Vancouver, etc.).

## Pitfalls
1. **False Positives/Negatives:** The LLM may label human content as AI-generated (False Positive) or fail to detect well-'humanized' AI content (False Negative).
2. **Overreliance:** Trusting the LLM as the sole plagiarism detector, ignoring the need for specialized tools and human review.
3. **Training Bias:** The LLM tends to identify as 'AI-generated' text that resembles its own training style, which may penalize clear and concise writing.
4. **Unethical Use:** Using prompts to 'bypass' AI detectors, which is an unethical practice and can lead to academic or professional sanctions.
5. **Context Limitation:** The inability to analyze very long documents at once, leading to partial or incomplete analyses.
