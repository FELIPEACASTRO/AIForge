# Lesson Planning Prompts

## Description
**Lesson Planning Prompts** are structured instructions provided to large language models (LLMs) to automate or assist in the creation of lesson plans, teaching units, activities, and support materials. This *prompt engineering* technique is widely used by educators to save time on administrative tasks and focus on pedagogy and student interaction. By providing specific details about the target audience (grade level), the topic, the learning objectives, and the output format, the AI can quickly generate lesson plan drafts, differentiated activity suggestions, assessment questions, and even grading rubrics. Its effectiveness lies in the teacher's ability to provide rich context and iterate on the result, transforming a generic draft into a personalized, high-quality lesson plan.

## Examples
```
1. **Detailed Lesson Plan:** "Act as a 9th-grade History teacher. Create a 50-minute lesson plan on the 'French Revolution'. Include: learning objective (based on the BNCC), required materials, engagement activity (hook), development (with differentiation for students with reading difficulties), and an exit assessment (exit ticket)."

2. **Creating Learning Objectives:** "For a 2nd-year High School Biology unit on 'Mendelian Genetics', generate 5 measurable learning objectives in the format 'The student will be able to...'. Include one objective that focuses on higher-order thinking skills (analysis or evaluation)."

3. **Project-Based Learning (PBL) Activity:** "Develop a 3-week Project-Based Learning (PBL) activity for 7th-grade students on 'Sustainability and Conscious Consumption'. The project should culminate in a presentation. Provide: the main challenge, the weekly milestones, and an assessment rubric for the final presentation."

4. **Content Differentiation:** "Take the following concept: [Insert a paragraph about 'Photosynthesis']. Now, rewrite it in three versions: 1) Simplified (for students with learning difficulties), 2) Standard (for most students), and 3) Advanced (focusing on oxidation-reduction reactions, for high-performing students)."

5. **Generating Assessment Questions:** "Create 10 multiple-choice questions and 2 essay questions for a test on 'Quadratic Functions' for 1st-year High School. The questions should have varying difficulty levels (Easy, Medium, Hard) and include the answer key with the justification for each answer."

6. **Suggesting Support Materials:** "For a Literature class on 'Brazilian Romanticism', suggest 3 multimedia resources (short videos, podcasts, or articles) that are appropriate for 3rd-year High School. For each resource, provide a brief summary and a discussion question for the classroom."

7. **Discussion Script:** "Create a script of 10 open-ended questions to facilitate a classroom discussion on the topic 'Ethics of Artificial Intelligence' for university students. The questions should stimulate debate and critical reflection on the social and moral implications of AI."
```

## Best Practices
**Be Specific and Contextualize:** Always include the grade level (e.g., 5th grade of Elementary School, 1st year of High School), the class time, the learning objectives, and the desired output format (e.g., table, list, detailed plan). **Use Personas:** Ask the AI to act as an "experienced instructional designer" or "teacher specialized in [subject]" to refine the quality and tone of the response. **Iteration and Refinement:** Use the first result as a draft. Ask the AI to refine, differentiate, or adapt the plan for different groups of students (e.g., students with special needs, advanced students). **Focus on Assessment:** Include prompts that generate assessment criteria, rubrics, and methods for measuring learning objectives. **Human Verification:** Always review the AI-generated plan to ensure factual accuracy, alignment with the local curriculum, and pedagogical appropriateness.

## Use Cases
**Rapid Draft Creation:** Quickly generate the basic structure of a lesson plan or teaching unit. **Teaching Differentiation:** Adapt the same content for different skill levels or learning styles within the same class. **Material Development:** Create vocabulary lists, multiple-choice questions, practice problems, and study guides. **Project-Based Learning (PBL):** Develop the scope, milestones, and assessment rubrics for complex projects. **Curriculum Alignment:** Generate learning objectives that comply with specific educational standards (e.g., BNCC in Brazil). **Substitution Preparation:** Create detailed lesson plans for substitute teachers (sub plans) with clear instructions and ready-to-use materials.

## Pitfalls
**Generic Generation:** The most common mistake is using vague prompts (e.g., "Create a lesson plan about history"), resulting in superficial plans with no curriculum alignment. **Bias and Inclusion:** The AI can perpetuate biases or generate plans that do not consider cultural diversity and students' inclusion needs. Human review is crucial. **Factual "Hallucinations":** The AI may include incorrect facts, non-existent resources, or outdated concepts. It is mandatory to verify the accuracy of the content. **Over-Reliance:** Complete reliance on AI can lead to the loss of pedagogical creativity and the teacher's disconnection from the real needs of their class. AI should be a tool, not a substitute. **Lack of Curriculum Context:** Failing to specify the curriculum (e.g., BNCC, Common Core) or teaching standards can result in plans misaligned with formal educational requirements.
