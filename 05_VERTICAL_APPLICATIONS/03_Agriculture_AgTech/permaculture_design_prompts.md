# Permaculture Design Prompts

## Description
**Permaculture Design Prompts** are structured instructions provided to artificial intelligence (AI) language models, such as ChatGPT or specialized tools, with the goal of generating design plans, strategies, and solutions aligned with the principles and ethics of permaculture [1].

Permaculture is an ecological design system that seeks to create sustainable and regenerative human environments, based on patterns and relationships found in nature. By integrating AI, the design process is accelerated and enhanced, allowing the AI to process large volumes of data (climate, soil, hydrology, biodiversity) to suggest the ideal allocation of elements (zones, structures, plants) on the land [2] [3].

These prompts are typically complex and multifaceted, requiring the user to define the geographic context, the project objectives (e.g., food production, water conservation, natural building), and the ethical constraints. The AI acts as a design assistant, helping with factor analysis, the suggestion of plant guilds, the planning of water harvesting systems (such as *swales* and dams), and the optimization of the system's energy efficiency [4]. The use of AI in permaculture design is a growing trend, especially for data analysis and the generation of "intelligent drafts" of designs, but it requires validation and adjustment by an experienced human designer [5].

## Examples
```
**1. Zone and Sector Design:**
`"Act as a permaculture designer. I have a 5-hectare plot of land in a Csa climate (Mediterranean, hot and dry) with clayey soil and a 15% south-facing slope. My main objective is food production for the family and maximum water conservation. Generate a detailed permaculture design plan, including: 1) Definition and location of the 5 permaculture zones. 2) Sector analysis (sun, wind, fire). 3) Suggestion of 3 water harvesting strategies (e.g., swales, keyline). 4) A list of 10 native and edible species suitable for Zone 1 and 2."`

**2. Creation of Plant Guilds:**
`"Create a plant guild for an apple tree (main tree) in a temperate climate (Cfb). The guild should include: 1) A nitrogen-fixing plant. 2) A mineral-accumulating plant. 3) A pest-repellent plant. 4) A ground-cover plant. 5) A plant that attracts beneficial insects. Provide the scientific name and function of each plant in the guild."`

**3. Water System Planning:**
`"Based on the principles of *Keyline Design*, develop a plan for the construction of *swales* on a 2-hectare plot with gently rolling topography. The goal is to maximize water infiltration and uniform distribution. The plan should include: 1) The approximate calculation of the spacing and length of the *swales*. 2) The type of vegetation to be planted on the embankment of each *swale* for stabilization and biomass production. 3) An estimate of the time to complete the work."`

**4. Energy Efficiency Analysis for Natural Building:**
`"Analyze the energy efficiency of an 80m² *cob* house (earth and straw) in a humid tropical climate (Af). The design should prioritize passive ventilation and cooling. Suggest 5 design features (e.g., orientation, roof type, thermal mass) that optimize thermal comfort without relying on air conditioning. Justify each suggestion based on the principles of permaculture."`

**5. Ecological Succession Strategy:**
`"Create a 5-year ecological succession plan to transform a degraded pasture area into a *Successional Agroforestry* system (SAF). The plan should detail: 1) The pioneer species (short-cycle plants). 2) The transition species (medium-cycle plants). 3) The climax species (fruit and timber trees). 4) The planting and management schedule (pruning and *chop-and-drop*)." `
```

## Best Practices
**1. Provide Detailed Context Data:** Include specific information about the site (climate, soil type, topography, area size, existing water resources) so that the AI can generate a relevant and accurate design.
**2. Define the Ethics and Principles:** Begin the prompt by specifying the three ethics of permaculture (Earth Care, People Care, Fair Share) and the design principles that should be prioritized (e.g., Catch and Store Energy, Integrate rather than Segregate).
**3. Use the Language of Permaculture:** Use technical terms such as "sectors", "zones", "swales", "plant guilds", "keyline design" to refine the AI's response.
**4. Ask for Structured Outputs:** Request that the AI organize the response in useful formats, such as tables, materials lists, or step-by-step action plans.
**5. Human Validation is Crucial:** Always use the AI's output as a starting point or an "intelligent draft". The final design should be validated and adjusted by a human designer with local knowledge and practical experience.

## Use Cases
**1. Rural and Urban Property Planning:** Generation of initial layouts (zones and sectors) for new permaculture projects, from small urban gardens to large farms [1].
**2. Water Resource Optimization:** Creation of detailed plans for rainwater harvesting, construction of *swales*, dams, and efficient irrigation systems, maximizing resilience to drought [4].
**3. Species Selection and Guild Creation:** Suggestion of native, edible, and functional plant species, and the design of *plant guilds* (beneficial associations) to increase biodiversity and productivity [2].
**4. Soil and Climate Analysis:** Processing of soil and climate data to recommend management strategies (e.g., green manure, composting, *mulching*) and the choice of the most suitable crops.
**5. Education and Training:** Use of AI to simulate design scenarios and provide instant feedback, serving as a didactic tool for students of Permaculture Design Courses (PDC) [5].

## Pitfalls
**1. Ignoring the Local Context (The "Cookie-Cutter" Error):** The AI may generate generic designs. The biggest mistake is applying the design without considering the nuances of the microclimate, soil, local culture, and regulations.
**2. Overestimating the AI's Capability:** The AI is an analysis and suggestion tool, not a substitute for human observation and interaction. The AI cannot feel the soil, observe the subtle flow of water, or interact with the local community.
**3. Data and Ethical Bias:** AI models are trained on existing data, which may have cultural or geographic biases, leading to suggestions that do not align with the ethics of permaculture (e.g., high energy consumption for infrastructure).
**4. Lack of Detail in the Prompt:** Vague prompts result in useless designs. The lack of specifications about the ethics, principles, climate zones, and project objectives will lead to superficial responses.
**5. Technological Dependence:** Permaculture values resilience and independence. Excessive reliance on digital tools can go against the principle of using renewable resources and natural services.
