# Pest Control Prompts

## Description
"Pest Control Prompts" is not a formally recognized prompt engineering technique (like CoT or Few-Shot), but rather a **thematic category** of prompts. These prompts are designed to be used by professionals, companies, or individuals interested in **Pest Control and Pest Management**. The goal is to leverage Large Language Models (LLMs) for industry-specific tasks, such as marketing, customer service, technical content generation, infestation data analysis, and treatment plan development.

## Examples
```
1. **Marketing/Advertising:** "Act as a digital marketing specialist for termite control services. Create a 30-second script for a video ad focused on homeowners in Florida, highlighting the 5-year warranty and the use of eco-friendly products."
2. **Customer Service (CSR):** "You are a customer support bot for a pest control company. A customer called to report that they saw a mouse in the kitchen. Provide an initial response script that demonstrates empathy, collects essential information (location, frequency, signs), and schedules a preliminary inspection."
3. **Technical Analysis:** "Analyze the following inspection data (German cockroach infestation, Level 4/5, Affected areas: Kitchen and master Bathroom, Products previously used: Spray A and Bait B without success). Recommend a 3-step treatment plan, including chemical rotation (active ingredients) and exclusion measures, justifying the choice."
4. **Content Generation:** "Create an educational post for social media (Instagram) about the prevention of Aedes aegypti mosquitoes. The post should be concise, use accessible language, and include 3 practical tips focused on eliminating standing water. Use relevant hashtags."
5. **Quote/Sales:** "Generate a professional quote for a routine residential pest control service (quarterly) for a 3-bedroom house. Include a description of the services, the price per visit, and a 10% discount for annual payment. The tone should be persuasive and focused on the value of prevention."
6. **Regulation/Safety:** "Explain, in simple terms, the safety guidelines for handling and disposing of rodenticide baits, in accordance with Brazilian regulations (ANVISA). The target audience is newly hired technicians."
```

## Best Practices
* **Define the Role (Role-Playing):** Always begin the prompt by defining the AI's role (e.g., "Act as a certified pest control technician", "You are the marketing manager").
* **Target Specificity:** Be extremely specific about the pest, the location (residential, commercial, agricultural), the product/method, and the goal of the output (e.g., "German cockroach", "Facebook ad", "insecticide rotation plan").
* **Safety and Ethics Constraints:** Include safety and compliance constraints (e.g., "The response must comply with ANVISA guidelines", "Do not recommend the use of illegal or dangerous products").
* **Output Structure:** Ask the AI to structure the output in specific formats (e.g., "Create a table with 3 columns", "Generate a 5-paragraph script").

## Use Cases
* **Marketing and Sales:** Creation of content for advertisements, social media posts, sales emails, and telemarketing scripts.
* **Training and Technical Support:** Generation of procedure guides, safety manuals, and training materials for new technicians.
* **Customer Service:** Development of chatbots and response scripts for frequently asked questions and customer complaints.
* **Treatment Planning:** Analysis of infestation data to recommend optimized treatment plans and chemical product rotation.
* **Regulatory Compliance:** Summaries and explanations of pest control laws and regulations.

## Pitfalls
* **Incorrect/Dangerous Information:** The AI may generate recommendations for products or methods that are dangerous, illegal, or ineffective. **Always verify the information with a certified professional.**
* **Over-Generalization:** The AI may provide generic responses that do not apply to the specific pest or geographic context.
* **Content Policy Violation:** Prompts that describe extermination methods in a very graphic way or that request the use of restricted substances may be refused by the AI.
* **Lack of Local Data:** The AI may not have access to localized infestation data or specific regulations of a particular city/state.
