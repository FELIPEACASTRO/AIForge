# Aquaculture Prompts

## Description
"Aquaculture Prompts" refers to the application of Prompt Engineering techniques to interact with Large Language Models (LLMs) and Vision-Language Models (VLMs) in the context of aquaculture and fisheries. The goal is to leverage Artificial Intelligence (AI) to optimize processes, improve sustainability, increase productivity, and manage the health of aquatic organisms. AI applications in aquaculture are vast, including real-time monitoring of water quality (pH, dissolved oxygen, temperature), early detection of disease in fish and shrimp (often via image analysis), optimization of feeding strategies to minimize waste and maximize growth, and even the design of recirculating aquaculture systems (RAS). The most recent trend (2024-2025) involves the integration of LLMs (such as the AQUA model) and RAG-LLMs with IoT (Internet of Things) systems and Computer Vision to create more sophisticated autonomous and decision-support systems.

## Examples
```
1. **Water Quality Analysis (Persona and Data Context):** "Act as a Water Quality Engineer. Analyze the following data from a tilapia tank in a RAS system: Temperature: 28.5°C, Dissolved Oxygen (DO): 4.2 mg/L, pH: 6.8, Total Ammonia: 0.5 ppm. Assess the risk of stress and suggest three immediate actions to optimize DO and reduce Ammonia while maintaining sustainability." 2. **Disease Diagnosis (Multimodal/VLM):** "Based on the attached image of a red drum showing white lesions on its fins and lethargy, act as a Fish Veterinarian. What is the most likely diagnosis? What are the treatment options with the least environmental impact and the recommended dosage for a 10,000-liter tank?" 3. **Feeding Optimization (Historical Data):** "Act as an Aquatic Nutrition Specialist. The current feed conversion ratio (FCR) for whiteleg shrimp (Litopenaeus vannamei) is 1.8. The total biomass is 5,000 kg. Based on the water temperature of 29°C and the feed consumption history of the last 48 hours (attached), propose a new feeding schedule for the next 24 hours that targets an FCR of 1.5, detailing the total quantity and distribution frequency." 4. **System Design (Engineering):** "Develop an engineering design outline for a Recirculating Aquaculture System (RAS) capable of producing 50 tonnes of Atlantic salmon per year. Include the main components (biofilter, mechanical filter, degasser, etc.), the required water flow (L/min), and an estimate of energy consumption (kWh/year)." 5. **Harvest Forecasting (Predictive Modeling):** "Based on the growth data (average weekly weight) and water temperature of the last 60 days for a population of 10,000 carp, predict the ideal harvest date to reach an average weight of 1.5 kg per fish. Present the forecast with a margin of error of 5 days and justify the predictive modeling methodology used." 6. **Inspection Automation (High-Level Instruction for LLM-ROV):** "Act as a Mission Planner. Convert the high-level instruction 'Inspect all mussel cultivation nets at Alpha Marine Farm' into a sequence of low-level commands for an autonomous ROV. The plan must include: 1) Optimized navigation route, 2) Camera parameters for biofouling detection, and 3) Anomaly logging protocol." 7. **Sustainability and Regulation Analysis:** "Evaluate the environmental impact of a new 20 tonnes/year tilapia farm in ponds, in accordance with the FAO Guidelines for Sustainable Aquaculture. What are the three main regulatory risks in Brazil (or in a specific country, if provided), and which effluent mitigation technologies (e.g., biofloc system) would you recommend to ensure compliance and ASC certification?"
```

## Best Practices
1. **Domain Specificity:** Use precise technical aquaculture terminology (e.g., "feed conversion ratio", "biofloc system", "dissolved oxygen"). 2. **Data Contextualization:** Include relevant input data (e.g., "pH levels are at 6.5, temperature at 28°C, and DO at 4.5 mg/L") so that the LLM can provide actionable analyses and recommendations. 3. **Role Definition (Persona):** Ask the model to act as an "Aquaculture Engineer", "Fish Veterinarian", or "Water Quality Specialist". 4. **Multimodal Integration:** For diagnostic or behavior monitoring tasks, specify that the prompt should be used together with a VLM (Vision-Language Model) that is analyzing an image or video. 5. **Sustainability Focus:** Include sustainability constraints in the prompts (e.g., "Minimize the use of antibiotics", "Reduce nitrogen effluent").

## Use Cases
1. **Water Quality Monitoring and Forecasting:** Use of prompts to analyze sensor (IoT) data and predict critical fluctuations in parameters such as dissolved oxygen, pH, and ammonia, enabling proactive interventions. 2. **Disease Detection and Diagnosis:** Prompts to analyze images or videos of aquatic organisms (fish, shrimp) and identify early signs of disease or stress, providing diagnoses and treatment recommendations. 3. **Feeding Optimization:** Use of prompts to analyze feeding behavior and environmental conditions, and generate ideal feeding schedules, reducing feed waste and improving the feed conversion ratio (FCR). 4. **System Design and Engineering:** Complex prompts to assist in the design of aquaculture systems, such as sizing Recirculating Aquaculture Systems (RAS) or optimizing tank layout. 5. **Automation of Inspection Tasks:** Integration of LLMs with underwater vehicles (ROVs) to process high-level instructions (e.g., "Inspect all fish nets") and generate detailed inspection plans.

## Pitfalls
1. **Treatment Hallucinations:** The LLM may suggest incorrect or outdated treatments or diagnoses. Always verify the recommendations with a human specialist. 2. **Lack of Specific Training Data:** General-purpose models may not have deep knowledge of specific aquatic species or aquaculture pathogens. 3. **Ignoring Local Context:** Recommendations may not be suitable for the local climate, regulations, or type of aquaculture system (e.g., freshwater vs. saltwater, pond vs. RAS). 4. **Overdependence on Sensors:** The prompt may be based on faulty or uncalibrated sensor data, leading to wrong decisions. The prompt should include a data validation step. 5. **Ambiguity in Terminology:** The use of generic terms can lead to imprecise responses. Technical precision is crucial.
