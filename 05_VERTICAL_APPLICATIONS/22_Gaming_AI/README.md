# 22 Gaming AI

> AI applied to interactive entertainment: agents that play games, generate playable content (PCG/PCGML), drive lifelike NPCs and dialogue, balance and test games, and detect cheating/toxicity — spanning a $180B+ industry and one of RL's most productive research testbeds.

## Why it matters

Games are the canonical sandbox for sequential decision-making: bounded rules, cheap simulation, and clear reward make them the proving ground where deep RL milestones (Atari, AlphaStar, OpenAI Five) were first established before transferring to robotics and operations research. On the production side, generative AI is reshaping content pipelines — procedural levels, generative NPC dialogue and faces, automated playtesting, and skill-based matchmaking — turning what was hand-authored into trainable, data-driven systems. The combination of LLM agents and game engines is now the leading platform for studying open-ended, embodied, lifelong learning.

## Categories

| Sub-area | What it covers | Representative tech |
|---|---|---|
| Game-playing agents (RL) | Agents that learn to play via reward maximization | AlphaStar, OpenAI Five, MuZero, DreamerV3 |
| LLM / embodied agents | Language-model agents acting in game worlds | Voyager, Generative Agents, SIMA |
| Procedural content generation (PCG/PCGML) | Levels, maps, quests, items generated by search/ML/LLMs | WaveFunctionCollapse, GANs, LLM PCG |
| Generative NPCs & dialogue | Talking, expressive non-player characters | NVIDIA ACE, Audio2Face-3D, Inworld |
| Generative assets | Textures, sprites, 3D models, audio | Diffusion / text-to-3D pipelines |
| Automated playtesting & QA | Agents that stress-test balance and find bugs | RL test bots, Unity ML-Agents |
| Matchmaking & skill rating | Ranking and pairing players | Elo, Glicko, TrueSkill |
| Anti-cheat & toxicity | Detecting cheating, smurfing, abuse | ML classifiers, behavior models |
