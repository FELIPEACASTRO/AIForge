# Behavioral Research & Poker Psychology

> The peer-reviewed behavioral-science literature that uses poker as a laboratory for decision-making under uncertainty (tomada de decisão sob incerteza): tilt and emotion regulation, loss-chasing, skill-vs-chance evidence, problem-gambling prevalence among players, and the efficacy of responsible-gambling tools — every study, book, journal and helpline below verified against a live source. Research and education only; this page is the responsible-gambling research anchor for the poker section.

---

## ⚠️ Responsible Gambling First (Jogo Responsável em Primeiro Lugar)

**This index is for research and education only — not an inducement to play for money.** Poker has a skill component (see evidence below), but it remains gambling: short-term outcomes are dominated by **variance (variância)**, and **rake (taxa da casa)** means the player pool as a whole loses money. The clinical literature on this very page documents tilt, loss-chasing and pathological gambling *among poker players specifically*. If gambling stops being fun, stop and seek help:

| Resource | Region / Language | What it offers | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|

## 1. Tilt, Emotions & Decision-Making Under Uncertainty

The single most poker-specific behavioral literature is on **tilt (perda de controle emocional)** — the Finnish group of **Jussi Palomäki, Michael Laakasuo and Mikko Salmela** (University of Helsinki) produced the core empirical series. All papers below are peer-reviewed and free to read at least in abstract form.

| Study | Finding / topic | Venue | Link |
|---|---|---|---|
| Palomäki, Laakasuo & Salmela (2013), *"'This is just so unfair!'"* | Qualitative analysis of loss-induced emotions and tilting in Finnish online players; moral indignation at "unfair" losses fuels tilt | International Gambling Studies | [tandfonline.com](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/14459795.2013.780631) |
| Palomäki, Laakasuo & Salmela (2013), *"Don't Worry, It's Just Poker!"* | Experience, self-rumination and self-reflection as determinants of decision-making quality online | Journal of Gambling Studies | [link.springer.com](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10899-012-9311-3) |
| Palomäki, Laakasuo & Salmela (2014), *Losing More by Losing It* | More experienced players report better emotion regulation after losses; sensitivity to losses predicts tilting severity | Journal of Gambling Studies | [researchportal.helsinki.fi](https://researchportal.helsinki.fi/en/publications/losing-more-by-losing-it-poker-experience-sensitivity-to-losses-a) |
| Laakasuo, Palomäki & Salmela (2014), *Experienced Poker Players Are Emotionally Stable* | Poker experience correlates with higher emotional stability | Cyberpsychology, Behavior, and Social Networking | [doi.org](https://doi.org/10.1089/cyber.2014.0147) |
| *Tilt in Online Poker: Loss of Control and Gambling Disorder* (2020) | In 291 online players, tilt-episode frequency and cognitive distortions were the significant predictors of excessive gambling — tilt as a possible gateway to disorder | Int. J. Environmental Research & Public Health (open access) | [doi.org](https://doi.org/10.3390/ijerph17145013) |

**Loss-chasing (perseguir perdas)** — a DSM-5 criterion for gambling disorder — has its own measurement literature: [*Winning and losing in online gambling: Effects on within-session chasing*](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0273359) (PLOS ONE, 2022; losses accelerated speed of play more than they extended sessions) and [*An Empirical Attempt to Operationalize Chasing Losses Utilizing Account-Based Player Tracking Data*](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC10628006/) (open access via PubMed Central). Free to read.

## 2. Skill, Expertise & Deliberate Practice Evidence

The skill-vs-chance (habilidade vs. sorte) question drives both legal classification and expertise research. Key verified empirical studies:

| Study | Finding / topic | Venue | Link |
|---|---|---|---|
| DeDonno & Detterman (2008), *Poker Is a Skill* | Participants given strategy instruction consistently outperformed controls across two experiments | Gaming Law Review 12:31–36 | [researchgate.net](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/233757178_Poker_Is_a_Skill) |
| Siler (2010), *Social and Psychological Challenges of Poker* | ~27M online hands: win-rate edges attenuate at higher stakes; tight-aggressive play spreads upward | Journal of Gambling Studies 26(3) | [link.springer.com](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10899-009-9168-2) |
| Levitt & Miles (2011), *The Role of Skill Versus Luck in Poker* | 2010 WSOP field data: a priori "skilled" players earned dramatically higher ROI than others | NBER Working Paper 17023 (free PDF) | [nber.org](https://www.nber.org/system/files/working_papers/w17023/w17023.pdf) |
| Meyer, von Meduna, Brosowski & Hayer (2013), *Is Poker a Game of Skill or Chance? A Quasi-Experimental Study* | Experts vs. average players on prearranged hands: card distribution dominated outcomes, but skill reduced losses on bad hands | Journal of Gambling Studies 29(3) | [link.springer.com](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10899-012-9327-8) |
| *Beyond Chance? The Persistence of Performance in Online Poker* (2015) | Large online samples show performance persistence — a skill signature chance games lack | PLOS ONE (open access) | [journals.plos.org](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0115479) |
| Leonard, Staples & Williams (2015), *Development and Validation of the Poker Skills Measure (PSM)* | Validated scenario-based skill test; scores predicted objective play performance in 100 participants | Journal of Gambling Studies 31(3):849–866 | [link.springer.com](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10899-014-9475-0) |

Together these support a **"skill matters, variance dominates short-run"** consensus — exactly why responsible-gambling framing is non-negotiable even for winning players. For the AI side of the skill question (Libratus, Pluribus, CFR), see this section's [Poker AI Milestones](Poker_AI_Milestones_and_Research.md) page.

## 3. Problem-Gambling Research on Poker Players (Pesquisa sobre Jogo Problemático)

Clinical work — much of it by **Servane Barrault & Isabelle Varescon** (France) — treats online poker players as a distinct at-risk population:

| Study | Finding / topic | Venue | Link |
|---|---|---|---|
| Barrault & Varescon (2013) | Among 245 online players (146 non-pathological / 55 problem / 44 pathological by SOGS): illusion of control, perceived inability to stop, depression and anxiety predicted problem-gambling scores | Cyberpsychology, Behavior, and Social Networking | [doi.org](https://doi.org/10.1089/cyber.2012.0150) |
| Barrault & Varescon (2017), *Anxiety, Depression and Emotion Regulation Among Regular Online Poker Players* | Emotion-regulation difficulties linked to gambling severity in regular players | Journal of Gambling Studies | [pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28105539/) |
| *Gambling Motives: Do They Explain Cognitive Distortions in Male Poker Gamblers?* (2017) | Motives (money, escape) partially explain distorted cognitions | Journal of Gambling Studies | [link.springer.com](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10899-017-9700-8) |
| *Psychopathology of Online Poker Players: Review of Literature* (2017) | Systematic review of comorbidity/psychopathology findings across poker studies | Open access via PubMed Central | [pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5387767/) |
| *Are Poker Players All the Same? Latent Class Analysis* (2014) | Player typologies with distinct risk profiles — "poker player" is not one population | Journal of Gambling Studies | [link.springer.com](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10899-013-9429-y) |
| *One-Year Prospective Study on Passion and Gambling Problems in Poker Players* (2018) | Obsessive (vs. harmonious) passion prospectively linked to gambling problems | Journal of Gambling Studies | [link.springer.com](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10899-017-9706-2) |
| *Poker Players With Experience and Skill Are Not "Ill"* | Argues standard problem-gambling screens misclassify skilled high-volume players — a measurement caution, not an all-clear | Journal of Gambling and Commercial Gaming Research | [journal.gamblingstudies.org](https://journal.gamblingstudies.org/index.php/jgcg/article/view/83) |

### Do responsible-gambling tools actually work? (Eficácia das ferramentas)

- **Auer & Griffiths (2016)**, [*Personalized Behavioral Feedback for Online Gamblers: A Real World Empirical Study*](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2016.01875/full) — Frontiers in Psychology, open access: real-world tracking data show personalized feedback and voluntary limit-setting reduce gambling intensity, especially in the highest-intensity players; generic pop-ups alone do less. Earlier companion study [also open access](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4585278/).
- **Cambridge Handbook of Substance and Behavioral Addictions**, ch. 27: [*Feedback Models for Gambling Control: The Use and Efficacy of Online Responsible Gambling Tools*](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/abs/cambridge-handbook-of-substance-and-behavioral-addictions/feedback-models-for-gambling-control-the-use-and-efficacy-of-online-responsible-gambling-tools/9F2609B94B66C500A712DED36F53E83B) — paid (institutional access); synthesizes the tool-efficacy evidence base.
- Brazil's regulator now **mandates** such tools: authorized `.bet.br` operators must offer self-limitation and time/spend alerts ([SPA/MF Jogo Responsável](https://www.gov.br/fazenda/pt-br/composicao/orgaos/secretaria-de-premios-e-apostas/jogo-responsavel)).

## 4. Crossover Books: Poker Psychology for General Readers

| Book | Topic | Author background | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| [*Thinking in Bets*](https://www.annieduke.com/books/) (Portfolio/Penguin, 2018) | Separating decision quality from outcome quality ("resulting"); probabilistic thinking from the felt to business | Annie Duke — WSOP bracelet winner (2004), graduate training in cognitive psychology | Paid |
| [*The Biggest Bluff*](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/562852/the-biggest-bluff-by-maria-konnikova/) (Penguin Press, 2020) | Psychology PhD learns no-limit hold'em from Erik Seidel as a study of skill, luck and attention; NYT bestseller | Maria Konnikova — psychologist and science writer | Paid |

Both are popular-science bridges into the academic literature above — useful for teaching decision-making under uncertainty without endorsing real-money play.
