# Regulation, Skill-vs-Chance Law & Poker Economics

> The legal and economic scaffolding around poker: the landmark courtroom fights over whether poker is a game of **skill (habilidade)** or **chance (sorte)**, the US federal and state-by-state regulatory landscape, 🇧🇷 Brazil's `Lei nº 14.790/2023` betting framework and poker's classification as a mind-sport, and the hard economics of **rake (taxa da casa)** that make the player pool a net loser. This is the *law-and-money* companion to this section's [Behavioral Research & Poker Psychology](Behavioral_Research_and_Poker_Psychology.md) page, which covers the *scientific* skill-vs-chance evidence. Every statute number, case citation, and date below is verified against a primary or official source. Research and education only — not legal or tax advice, and not an inducement to play for money.

## 1. Skill vs. Chance in the Courtroom (Habilidade vs. Sorte nos Tribunais)

Whether poker is "predominantly skill" is not just an academic question — it decides whether a game is legal *gambling* and who may run it. The most-cited modern US test case is **United States v. DiCristina**.

| Ruling | Court / Citation | Date | Holding |
|---|---|---|---|
| **US v. DiCristina** (district) | E.D.N.Y., **886 F. Supp. 2d 164**; Judge Jack B. Weinstein | **Aug 21, 2012** | The Illegal Gambling Business Act (IGBA), **18 U.S.C. § 1955**, reaches only games where *chance predominates*; on the expert record, **skill predominates in Texas Hold'em**, so a poker room is not an "illegal gambling business." Indictment dismissed; **judgment of acquittal**. |
| **US v. DiCristina** (appeal) | 2d Cir., **726 F.3d 92**, Docket 12-3720 | **Aug 6, 2013** | **Reversed.** The IGBA's list of covered activities is non-exhaustive ("includes but is not limited to"); the statute's three elements — state-law violation, 5+ persons, and 30+ days or $2,000/day revenue — say nothing about skill vs. chance. Because poker is illegal gambling **under New York state law**, the federal act applies **regardless of the skill question**. Conviction reinstated. |
| Supreme Court | Cert. petition No. 13-564 | **2014** | **Certiorari denied** — the Second Circuit reversal stands. |

**The lesson (a lição):** Weinstein's 120-page opinion remains the most thorough judicial treatment of the empirical skill argument, but the Second Circuit made it moot for *federal* IGBA purposes — federal liability piggybacks on **state** gambling law, so poker's "skill" status only matters where a *state* statute uses a predominance test. Primary reading: the district opinion on [Leagle](https://www.leagle.com/decision/infdco20120822871) and the appellate opinion on [FindLaw](https://caselaw.findlaw.com/court/us-2nd-circuit/1641292.html).

## 2. The US Federal Landscape (Panorama Federal dos EUA)

| Instrument | Citation | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **Federal Wire Act** | 18 U.S.C. § 1084 (1961) | Bars use of wire communications for certain bets. A 2011 DOJ Office of Legal Counsel opinion read it to cover **sports betting only**, which opened the door to state-licensed online poker/casino. |
| **UIGEA** — Unlawful Internet Gambling Enforcement Act of 2006 | **31 U.S.C. §§ 5361–5367** | Passed as Title VIII of the SAFE Port Act. Prohibits gambling *businesses* from **knowingly accepting payments** connected to bets "unlawful under any federal or state law." It targets **payment processing**, not individual players. |
| **"Black Friday"** — DOJ indictment | S.D.N.Y., unsealed **April 15, 2011** | DOJ indicted 11 people tied to the three largest sites serving US players — **PokerStars, Full Tilt Poker, Absolute Poker** — for bank/wire fraud, money laundering and illegal gambling. Domains were seized and replaced with FBI notices, ending US-facing online poker overnight. |
| Black Friday settlements | 2012 | PokerStars settled by forfeiting **$547 million** and acquiring Full Tilt's assets to repay players; DOJ characterized Full Tilt as having operated as a Ponzi-like scheme owing players ~$300M+. |

UIGEA text and the SAFE Port Act placement are documented on [Wikipedia (UIGEA)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unlawful_Internet_Gambling_Enforcement_Act_of_2006); the federal criminal-law overview is in the Congressional Research Service report [*Internet Gambling: An Overview of Federal Criminal Law* (97-619)](https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/97-619).

## 3. State-by-State Regulated Online Poker & the MSIGA Compact

After the 2011 Wire Act reinterpretation, regulation devolved to individual states. As of 2026, six states have **live, licensed** real-money online poker, and all six share liquidity through the **Multi-State Internet Gaming Agreement (MSIGA)** — an interstate compact that pools players so tables fill and prize pools grow.

| State | Regulator | Notable licensed rooms (2026) | In MSIGA? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Delaware** | Delaware Lottery | State-lottery platform (first US state to regulate, 2012) | ✅ (founding, 2014) |
| **Nevada** | Nevada Gaming Control Board / Commission | WSOP.com | ✅ (founding, 2014) |
| **New Jersey** | NJ Division of Gaming Enforcement | WSOP, BetMGM, Borgata, PokerStars | ✅ (joined 2017) |
| **Michigan** | Michigan Gaming Control Board | WSOP MI, BetMGM, PokerStars MI, BetRivers | ✅ (joined 2022) |
| **West Virginia** | WV Lottery | BetRivers Poker (real-money launch June 2025; in shared pool) | ✅ (joined 2023) |
| **Pennsylvania** | PA Gaming Control Board | Multiple (regulated since 2017 law) | ✅ (first shared tables **April 2025**) |

**MSIGA timeline (verified):** Nevada + Delaware signed the first shared-liquidity agreement in **2014**; New Jersey joined in **2017** (technical go-live for NJ shared tables **May 2018**); Michigan joined in **2022**; West Virginia joined in **2023** and launched real-money online poker (BetRivers) in **June 2025**; Pennsylvania's first interstate tables launched in **April 2025**. Sources: [Pokerfuse multi-state guide](https://pokerfuse.com/online-poker/united-states/us-multi-state-online-poker/), [CSG National Center for Interstate Compacts](https://compacts.csg.org/compact/multi-state-internet-gaming-agreement/).

## 4. UK, Germany & the EU (Reino Unido, Alemanha e UE)

Most of Europe classifies poker as **gambling / a game of chance** for *licensing* purposes even while acknowledging a skill element — the opposite emphasis from the DiCristina district court.

| Jurisdiction | Classification | Framework |
|---|---|---|
| **United Kingdom** | **Game of chance** (chance + skill) under the Gambling Act 2005 | "Equal chance" poker (e.g. Texas Hold'em, players against each other) is regulated; "unequal chance" poker where a banker holds a mathematical edge is casino-only. Overseen by the **Gambling Commission**. [Gambling Act 2005 (legislation.gov.uk)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2005/19/pdfs/ukpga_20050019_en.pdf) · [Poker under the Act (Spelthorne BC)](https://www.spelthorne.gov.uk/article/17963/Poker-under-the-Gambling-Act-2005) |
| **Germany** | **Glücksspiel** (game of chance) | The **Glücksspielstaatsvertrag 2021 (GlüStV 2021)** took effect **July 1, 2021**, legalizing licensed online poker under strict rules: **only variants without a banker**, **random seating**, and a **€1,000/month** cross-operator deposit limit. Regulated by the **GGL (Gemeinsame Glücksspielbehörde der Länder)**. [GGL — gesetzliche Regelungen](https://www.gluecksspiel-behoerde.de/de/fuer-gluecksspielanbieter/gesetzliche-regelungen) · [DLA Piper — State Treaty on Gambling 2021](https://www.dlapiper.com/en/insights/publications/2021/05/state-treaty-on-gambling-2021) |
| **EU (general)** | No single EU-wide gambling law | Gambling is left to member-state competence; operators must be licensed per country. Poker's skill/chance label therefore varies by national statute. |

## 5. 🇧🇷 Brazil: The Bets Law, .bet.br & Poker as a Mind-Sport

Brazil regulated online betting only recently, and — critically for poker — it distinguishes **fixed-odds betting (apostas de quota fixa)** from skill-based games.

- **`Lei nº 14.790, de 29 de dezembro de 2023`** (the "Lei das Bets") regulates **apostas de quota fixa** — betting on real sporting events and online fixed-odds games — amending `Lei nº 13.756/2018`. It assigns regulatory competence to the **Ministério da Fazenda**. Consolidated text on [Planalto](https://www.planalto.gov.br/ccivil_03/_ato2023-2026/2023/lei/L14790.htm); official landing page: [SPA/MF — Apostas de Quota Fixa](https://www.gov.br/fazenda/pt-br/composicao/orgaos/secretaria-de-premios-e-apostas/apostas-de-quota-fixa).
- **SPA — Secretaria de Prêmios e Apostas** is the central regulator created under the law; the national regulated market went live **January 1, 2025**. Only SPA-authorized companies may operate, each authorization allows up to **three brands**, and every legal operator uses the **`.bet.br`** domain. Authorization rules were set by **Portaria SPA/MF nº 827/2024** (de 21 de maio de 2024).
- **Poker's status.** Under the Bets Law, poker is **not** a fixed-odds bet, so it is not regulated as a `.bet.br` product. Domestically, poker's push for recognition is led by the **CBTH — Confederação Brasileira de Texas Hold'em** (founded January 29, 2009). On **November 16, 2024**, poker was recognized as a **mind-sport (esporte da mente)** by the **International Mind Sports Association (IMSA)** — the body that also recognizes chess, bridge, checkers and go — via the CBTH-co-founded **World Poker Federation (WPF)**. Source: [CBTH — "Poker é um Esporte da Mente"](https://cbth.org.br/poker-e-um-esporte-da-mente/).

> ⚠️ Note on names: the confederation's registered name is **Confederação Brasileira de Texas Hold'em (CBTH)**, not "de Truco e Poker." Mind-sport recognition (IMSA) is a *sporting* classification and does **not** by itself make real-money poker a licensed activity in Brazil.

## 6. Rake Economics & the House-Edge Math (Economia do Rake)

Poker differs from house-banked games (roulette, blackjack) in a subtle but decisive way: **players compete against each other, not the house.** The operator does not need an edge on outcomes — it profits by **charging rake** on the money that changes hands. This is why, *as a group*, players cannot break even.

**The negative-sum identity.** In any raked poker economy over time:

```
Σ(player results) = − Σ(rake + fees + tips)
```

The pool's *collective* expected value is exactly **negative the rake**. Individual winners exist, but only by beating both their opponents **and** the rake — a winning player's edge must exceed their rake burden to show profit.

**Cash-game rake** has two parameters:

| Parameter | Typical online value | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Percentage | ~**3%–5%** of the pot | Fraction skimmed from each raked pot |
| Cap (teto) | ~**$3–$5** | Maximum rake per pot, so big pots aren't over-raked |
| "No flop, no drop" | — | No rake is taken if the hand ends before the flop |

Worked example: at 5% with a $3 cap, a $200 pot is raked the flat **$3** (5% would be $10, but the cap binds), so $197 goes to the winner. Reference: [Rake (poker) — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rake_(poker)) · [GTO Wizard — Rake & Rakeback Explained](https://blog.gtowizard.com/rake-rakeback-explained-optimize-your-poker-earnings/) · [PrimeDope rake comparison](https://www.primedope.com/online-poker-rake-comparison-rake-calculator/).

**Why volume matters to the house:** a typical hand lasts 1–2 minutes; across dozens of simultaneous tables that is thousands of raked pots per hour — small per-pot skims compounding into the operator's revenue. From the player's side, rake is a constant tax on volume that must be modeled in any win-rate estimate.

## 7. Tournament Fee Structures (Estrutura de Taxas de Torneio)

Tournaments express rake differently — as an explicit **fee added to the buy-in**, shown with a "+" in the advertised price.

- A **`$100 + $10`** tournament sends **$100 to the prize pool** and **$10 to the house** as the tournament fee.
- Fees commonly run **~5%–20%** of the buy-in depending on stakes and format (higher on small/turbo events, lower on high buy-ins).
- The prize-pool portion is redistributed among finishers per the payout structure; the fee is pure operator revenue.

So the effective "rake" of a $100+$10 event is `10 / 110 ≈ 9.1%` of what you paid. Reference: [Pokercode — What Is Rake](https://www.pokercode.com/blog/poker-rake) · [Rake (poker) — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rake_(poker)).

## 8. Taxation of Winnings by Jurisdiction (Tributação dos Ganhos)

Tax treatment is jurisdiction-specific; the two most relevant to this index are the US and Brazil.

### 🇺🇸 United States — Form W-2G

Gambling winnings are taxable income; poker tournaments have a **specific reporting rule** on **Form W-2G, "Certain Gambling Winnings."**

| Item | Rule (per IRS *Instructions for Forms W-2G and 5754*, Rev. Jan 2026) |
|---|---|
| Poker-tournament reporting trigger | Net winnings (**winnings minus the buy-in/wager**) that **meet or exceed $5,000**. The 2026 revision keeps the poker-tournament threshold at **$5,000**. (The separate reporting threshold that was raised to **$2,000 and inflation-indexed** — up from $1,200 on slots and $1,500 on bingo — applies to **slot-machine and bingo** winnings, **not** to poker tournaments.) |
| Backup withholding | If the winner **fails to furnish a valid TIN**, the payer must apply **24% backup withholding** to the full winnings. |
| Winner's copy | Copy B due to the winner by **Jan 31**; IRS e-file by **Mar 31**. |

Primary source: [IRS — Instructions for Forms W-2G and 5754](https://www.irs.gov/instructions/iw2g) · form: [Form W-2G (Rev. Jan 2026)](https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-pdf/fw2g.pdf).

### 🇧🇷 Brazil — Imposto de Renda

- **Fixed-odds bets (`.bet.br`):** net prizes are taxed at **15%** on the annual net gain that **exceeds the first band of the progressive IRPF table** (that exempt band is **R$ 28.467,20**). The bettor self-assesses annually, aggregating gains and losses across operators, and declares under the "exclusive/definitive taxation" schedule. Rules detailed by the Receita Federal in **IN RFB nº 2.299/2025** (de 17 de dezembro de 2025). Reference: [Souto Correa — client alert on IN RFB 2.299/2025](https://www.soutocorrea.com.br/client-alerts/receita-federal-define-regras-de-tributacao-para-premios-de-apostas-de-quota-fixa-e-do-fantasy-sport-in-rfb-no-2-299-2025/).
- **Poker (não é aposta de quota fixa):** because live/tournament poker is **not** a fixed-odds bet, it falls outside the 15% `.bet.br` prize regime; poker earnings are generally treated as **ordinary income** and taxed under the **progressive IRPF table**. This is a materially different treatment from the fixed-odds prize rule — consult a Brazilian tax professional for your situation.
