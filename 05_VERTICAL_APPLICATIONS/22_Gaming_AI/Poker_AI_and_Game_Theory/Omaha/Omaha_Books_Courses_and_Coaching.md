# Omaha Books, Courses & Coaching (Livros, Cursos e Coaching de Omaha)

A fact-checked, deep catalog of the **human-study** side of Omaha — the printed **book canon** for **Pot-Limit Omaha (PLO)**, **5-/6-card PLO** and **Omaha Hi-Lo**, the modern **video training courses**, and the **coaches/authors** who built the field. This is the reading-and-lessons companion to the sibling [Omaha Strategy & GTO](./Omaha_Strategy_and_GTO.md) and [Omaha Solvers & Software](./Omaha_Solvers_and_Software.md) pages; it goes **much** deeper on *what to read and who to learn from* than the brief [Poker Variants](../Poker_Variants_PLO_and_Mixed_Games.md) overview. Every book title, author, publisher, year, ISBN, course and price below was verified live against a bookseller, publisher or course page in **July 2026**; items that could not be confirmed against a primary source were **dropped, not guessed**. For **research, education and off-table study only.**

> **The one-sentence buying guide (o guia de compra em uma frase):** start with a *transition/fundamentals* book to unlearn Hold'em instincts (Hwang's **Big Play Strategy** or Tri Nguyen's **The Pot-Limit Omaha Book**), add a *modern small-stakes* text (**JNandez — Mastering Small Stakes PLO**), then — only if PLO becomes your main game — invest in a *video course* (Upswing **PLO Launch Pad** at the low end, Galfond's **This Is PLO** at the deep end) and drill ranges in a trainer; books teach the *why*, courses and trainers teach the *reps*, and neither makes a structurally high-variance game beatable at stakes your bankroll can't absorb.

## Responsible Gambling First (Jogo Responsável)

> **Omaha is normally played for real money and its variance is *structurally higher* than No-Limit Hold'em — preflop equities run closer together, pots grow faster under pot-limit, and bankroll swings are larger (PLO standard deviation typically runs well above NLHE at the same stake, and split-pot Hi-Lo adds "getting quartered" on top). After rake, the player pool loses net.** Buying every book and course on this page does not make Omaha beatable if you play stakes your bankroll cannot absorb. ## How to Read This Page (como usar esta página)

Omaha learning material comes in three layers, and they are complements, not substitutes:

1. **Books (livros)** — the cheapest, densest way to install a *mental model*: why Omaha ≠ Hold'em, nut-focus, redraws, blockers, pot-limit sizing, hand selection. A US$20–35 book still outperforms hours of scattered videos for building foundations.
2. **Courses (cursos)** — structured video series (8 to 70+ hours) that show *worked hands* and modern, solver-informed lines, usually with quizzes and a community. Priced from ~US$99 to ~US$2,500.
3. **Coaching & trainers (coaching e treinadores)** — one-on-one coaching, membership communities, and GTO **drill trainers** (catalogued in depth on the [Omaha Solvers & Software](./Omaha_Solvers_and_Software.md) page) that turn theory into repetitions.

Everything below is **off-table study**. None of it is a substitute for bankroll discipline in a high-variance game.

### The classic era — Ciaffone, Reuben, Slotboom (a era clássica)

Before PLO had solvers, it had a handful of European- and cash-game-rooted texts. **Bob Ciaffone's** *Omaha Poker* is the elder statesman — first written in 1984 as *Omaha Holdem Poker* and rewritten as *Omaha Poker* in 2006 (adding ~20 pages on the pot-limit Omaha-high form) — a foundations-and-hand-values reference rather than a modern strategy manual. **Stewart Reuben's** *How Good is Your Pot-Limit Omaha?* (D&B, 2004) is a problem-set book that drills judgment through worked scenarios. **Rolf Slotboom's** *Secrets of Professional Pot-Limit Omaha* (D&B, 2006) was, for its time, an unusually candid look at medium/high-stakes cash PLO — a **short-stack approach**, bet-size study, seating, and the **bare-ace bluff / blocker** ideas that remain relevant today; his follow-up with **Rob Hollink**, *Secrets of Short-handed Pot-Limit Omaha* (D&B, 2009), extends this to short-handed cash. Treat these as **historical foundations**: correct on structure and nut-focus, but pre-dating solver-era range and blocker theory. Browse the current D&B PLO catalog at **[dandbpoker.com/collections/pot-limit-omaha](https://dandbpoker.com/collections/pot-limit-omaha)**.

### The Hwang era — the modern reference (a era Hwang)

**Jeff Hwang** is the author most responsible for turning PLO into a *studied* game for English-speaking players. **[*Pot-Limit Omaha Poker: The Big Play Strategy*](https://isbnsearch.org/isbn/9780818407260)** (Kensington's Lyle Stuart imprint, 2008) is still the most-recommended *first* PLO book — it teaches a draw-centric "big play" model built around **straight/wrap draws and nut-flush draws**, and a basic postflop framework. He followed it with the three-volume **Advanced Pot-Limit Omaha** series from **Dimat Enterprises**: **[Vol. I — *Small Ball and Short-Handed Play*](https://isbnsearch.org/isbn/9780974150291)** (2009), **[Vol. II — *LAG Play*](https://isbnsearch.org/isbn/9780984619405)** (2010), and **[Vol. III — *The Short-Handed Workbook*](https://isbnsearch.org/isbn/9780984619412)** (2010), which drills the theory through hundreds of worked hands. The trilogy is aimed at players moving beyond the basics into short-handed, aggressive cash play.

### The transition & aggressive era (a era da transição e agressão)

**Tri "SlowHabit" Nguyen's** **[*The Pot-Limit Omaha Book: Transitioning from NLHE to PLO*](https://isbnsearch.org/isbn/9781937101169)** (DailyVariance Publishing; first published 2009, this ISBN 2012) is the canonical **"I already play Hold'em — now teach me PLO"** text: it emphasizes **equity realization, fold equity, and turn/river bluffing**, and is frequently prescribed to NLHE players crushing small/mid-stakes. **Herbert Okolowitz & Wladimir Taschner's** **[*Mastering Pot-Limit Omaha: The Modern Aggressive Approach*](https://dandbpoker.com/products/mastering-pot-limit-omaha)** (D&B Publishing, 2014) pushed the field toward wide 3-/4-bet ranges and **exploiting equity shifts across turn and river**, connecting preflop decisions to later-street aggression.

### The modern small-stakes era (a era moderna de small stakes)

**Matthias Pum's** *Strategies to Beat Small Stakes Pot-Limit Omaha: From Beginner to Winner in 28 Lessons* (D&B Publishing, 2016) is a structured, lesson-by-lesson curriculum with exercises and solutions. The current standard-bearer, though, is **Fernando "JNandez" Habegger's** **[*Mastering Small Stakes Pot-Limit Omaha*](https://dandbpoker.com/products/mastering-small-stakes-pot-limit-omaha-how-to-crush-modern-plo-games)** (D&B Publishing, 2020, 336 pp.), which organizes preflop into **nine hand categories** and postflop into the **"Four Pillars of Postflop Play,"** with chapters on live play, PLO tournaments, bankroll and variance — written by a high-stakes pro who also runs the **PLO Mastermind** training site. His newer **[*100 Essential Tips to Master Pot-Limit Omaha*](https://dandbpoker.com/products/100-essential-tips-to-master-pot-limit-omaha)** (D&B Publishing, 2026, pre-order) distills modern concepts — stack size, position, blockers, hand structure — into tip-and-quiz form.

### Run It Once — Phil Galfond's platform (a plataforma do Galfond)

**[Run It Once](https://www.runitonce.com/)**, founded by **Phil Galfond**, hosts the deepest PLO course in existence: **[This Is PLO](https://www.runitonce.com/courses/this-is-plo/)** — **183 videos and 73+ hours** ($2,499 lifetime) walking through more than a dozen game-tree segments (dynamic, straight, flush and paired boards), fundamentals (MDF, calling ranges), and real hand reviews from Galfond's own high-stakes/heads-up play, with a 6-max section co-taught by **Richard Gryko**. For solver methodology across both games there is **Simplifying Solvers** ($999, NLHE + PLO), and the broader **RIO Elite** subscription library spans 9,500+ videos across formats. *(Note: Galfond's cheap **Foundations** course, $49.99, is an **NLHE** fundamentals course — not PLO — so don't buy it expecting Omaha content.)*

### Upswing Poker — the structured ladder (a escada estruturada)

**[Upswing Poker](https://upswingpoker.com/pot-limit-omaha-courses/)** offers the clearest *price ladder* in PLO, taught chiefly by high-stakes pros **Dylan Weisman** and **Chris Wehner** ("bluffmast"): the **$99 [PLO Launch Pad](https://upswingpoker.com/plo-launch-pad/)** (8+ hrs, beginner/low-mid), the **$999 [Advanced PLO Mastery](https://upswingpoker.com/product/advanced-plo-mastery/)** (36 hrs, cash), plus dedicated **Crushing PLO Tournaments** ($849) and **Live PLO Dominance** ($499) courses and the **PLO Matrix** preflop tool ($49/mo). Launch Pad → Advanced Mastery is a natural progression for a serious low-to-mid-stakes cash player.

### PLO Mastermind & trainers (PLO Mastermind e treinadores)

**[PLO Mastermind](https://plomastermind.com/)** — run by **JNandez** — is a membership *platform* (not a one-off course) with separate **4-Card, 5-Card and 6-Card** tracks, a **GTO Trainer**, quizzes and a coaching community, plus a genuinely free tier (a free "Preflop Pass" with 100bb 6-max preflop charts and a free PLO equity calculator, no credit card required). Its pricing and free-tier contents are documented on the sibling **[Omaha Solvers & Software](./Omaha_Solvers_and_Software.md)** page, alongside **Vision GTO Trainer**, **GTO Wizard PLO**, **PLO Genius** and **RangeConverter** — the *drill/solve* tools that turn course theory into repetitions.

## Caveats — How to Use These Resources (ressalvas)

- **Books age unevenly.** The *structural* lessons (nut-focus, redraws, blockers, pot-limit sizing, hand selection) hold up; specific *range* recommendations from pre-solver books (Ciaffone, Reuben, early Slotboom, even 2008-era Hwang) predate modern GTO ranges. Read old books for the *model*, calibrate ranges with modern tools.
- **Course prices change; verify before buying.** Every price here was live in July 2026 but poker courses re-price, discount and bundle constantly — reconfirm on the vendor page.
- **A course is not a substitute for reps.** Watching 70 hours of video without drilling ranges and reviewing your own hands produces recognition, not skill. Pair any course with a **trainer** (see [Solvers & Software](./Omaha_Solvers_and_Software.md)) and honest database review (see [Tracking Software, HUDs & Analytics](../Tracking_Software_HUDs_and_Analytics.md)).
- **Study ≠ live assistance.** Everything here is for learning *away* from the tables. Using a solver or trainer as **Real-Time Assistance (RTA)** during real-money play is banned and gets accounts closed and funds seized.
- **Variance is the real syllabus.** No book or coach removes PLO's structurally higher variance; bankroll and stake selection decide whether your edge survives the swings.
