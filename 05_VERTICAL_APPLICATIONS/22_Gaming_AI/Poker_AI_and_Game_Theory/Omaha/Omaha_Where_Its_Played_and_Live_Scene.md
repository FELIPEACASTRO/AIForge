# Omaha — Where It Is Played & Live Scene (Onde se Joga Omaha & o Cenário ao Vivo)

A fact-checked map of the **Pot-Limit Omaha (PLO)** ecosystem in 2026 — which online rooms currently spread Omaha, where the nosebleed online action lives, the live high-roller circuit (**Triton PLO**, **WSOP** Omaha bracelet events), the biggest streamed cash games (**Hustler Casino Live**), the modern "PLO boom," and the 🇧🇷 **Brazilian** Omaha scene. This page catalogues *where the games are played and who plays them*; the mechanics live on the sibling [Omaha Variants & Rules](./Omaha_Variants_and_Rules.md) page and the theory on [Omaha Strategy & GTO](./Omaha_Strategy_and_GTO.md). For **research, education and off-table study only.** Every site, event, field size and payout below was verified live in July 2026 against primary or first-party coverage; unverifiable items were dropped.

> **The Omaha ecosystem in one sentence (o ecossistema do Omaha em uma frase):** PLO is now the clear **#2 game in poker after No-Limit Hold'em** — spread around the clock online (led by **GGPoker**, plus **PokerStars**, **partypoker**, **ACR** and **888poker**), staged for seven-figure prizes on the **Triton** super-high-roller circuit and across a deep **WSOP** Omaha schedule, and streamed at **$500/$500/$1,000** nosebleeds where a single pot has topped **$1,000,000** — a boom that also carries the game's *structurally higher variance* to every stake.

## Responsible Gambling First (Jogo Responsável)

> **Everything on this page is real-money gambling, and Omaha's variance is *structurally higher* than No-Limit Hold'em — preflop equities run closer together, pots grow faster under pot-limit, and bankroll swings are larger (PLO standard deviation typically runs well above NLHE at the same stake; split-pot Hi-Lo adds "getting quartered" on top). ## The Modern "PLO Boom" (o boom do PLO)

Pot-Limit Omaha's share of the poker ecosystem has climbed sharply. The clearest recent evidence is that **at equal buy-ins, PLO is now out-drawing No-Limit Hold'em** at the game's flagship series:

- **2025 WSOP $5,000 Pot-Limit Omaha:** **757 total entries**, prize pool **over $3.5 million** — up from the **733** entries of the 2024 edition, and notably **more than the 693 entrants** in a same-priced **$5,000 no-limit hold'em** event ([CardPlayer](https://www.cardplayer.com/poker-news/wsop-5k-pot-limit-omaha-outpaces-5k-no-limit-holdem)). As that coverage put it, "**The great game of pot-limit Omaha is enjoying a surge in popularity of late.**"
- **2024 WSOP $1,000 Mystery Bounty PLO:** a then-record **4,280 entries** — one of the largest live PLO fields ever assembled — for a **$3,616,800** prize pool ($1,284,000 of it in mystery bounties); **Sascha Wilhelm** won his first bracelet for **$282,290** ([CardPlayer](https://www.cardplayer.com/poker-news/29139-sascha-wilhelm-wins-2024-wsop-pot-limit-omaha-mystery-bounty)).
- **High-roller adoption:** brand-new **$100,000** and existing **$25,000 / $50,000** PLO high rollers now anchor the WSOP schedule (below), and **Triton** has made PLO a fixture of its super-high-roller stops (further below).

Drivers commonly cited: PLO delivers **more action and bigger pots** per hand than NLHE; solver-era tools (GTO Wizard PLO, PLO Genius, MonkerSolver — see [Omaha Solvers & Software](./Omaha_Solvers_and_Software.md)) have made the game **studyable**; and the format is heavily promoted online (GGPoker's Omaholic, 888's PLO5/PLO6 launch, ACR's PLO bonuses). PLO is widely regarded as **the second most popular format after no-limit hold'em.**

## Live High-Roller PLO — Triton Poker Series (PLO de alto nível ao vivo)

The **[Triton Poker Series](https://triton-series.com/)** (founded **2016**, minimum buy-in **US$15,000**) is the premier super-high-roller tour, and it has fully embraced PLO. Across 2016–2026 Triton staged **300+ events awarding roughly US$1.88 billion**, drawing ~2,000 distinct players from 87 countries ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triton_Poker_Series)).

- **PLO is a standing part of the schedule**, not a novelty. Recent stops have run **PLO Main Events** and multiple dedicated PLO tournaments at **$25,000, $50,000 and $100,000** buy-ins — e.g. **Jeju (Mar 2025)** crowned **four PLO champions**, headlined by a record **$100,000 PLO Main Event** (**91 entries** — the largest six-figure-buy-in PLO field in history — won by **Sergio Martinez**), alongside **$25K, $50K and a $30K-bounty** PLO event; the **Monte Carlo (Nov 2024)** stop ran a **PLO Main Event** plus **$50K PLO (6-Handed)** and **$25K PLO** events ([PokerNews — Triton Jeju PLO](https://www.pokernews.com/news/2025/03/another-record-field-triton-jeju-crowns-four-plo-champions-48169.htm)).
- Full Triton results (including its PLO events) are catalogued on the official Triton site, with live-stream stats at [tritonpoker.plus](https://tritonpoker.plus/).

Triton's overall records (the **Triton Million** and Bryn Kenney's runner-up payout) are covered on the [Live Tournament Data & Results](../Live_Tournament_Data_and_Results.md) page and not duplicated here.

The streams are entertainment and study material — **not** a template for bankroll sizing.

## Brazil — the PLO Scene (🇧🇷 o cenário do PLO no Brasil)

Brazil is one of poker's fastest-growing markets, and PLO has an established, growing live and online presence.

- **BSOP (Brazilian Series of Poker)** — Latin America's largest live circuit — spreads dedicated Omaha events. The **BSOP Millions 2024** schedule included **"The Pot Limit Omaha Challenge" (Event #06)** at a **R$15,000** buy-in, drawing **43 players / 65 entries**, with **R$279,000** to the champion and a **R$25,800** min-cash ([SuperPoker](https://superpoker.com.br/amp/noticias/bsop-millions-mesa-final-do-the-pot-limit-omaha-challenge-e-formada)). BSOP stops also spread **5-Card PLO ("Omaha 5 Cartas")** and lower-buy-in PLO side events (roughly **R$1,500–R$2,500**), with Omaha's Brazilian footprint carrying into the 2026 season ([BSOP official](https://bsop.com.br/)).
- **Online:** Brazilian players access PLO through the same international rooms above (GGPoker's Omaholic, PokerStars, 888poker's PLO5/PLO6). Under Brazil's regulated betting framework, licensed operators use the **`.bet.br`** domain and must provide player-protection tooling ([Jogo Responsável](https://www.gov.br/fazenda/pt-br/composicao/orgaos/secretaria-de-premios-e-apostas/jogo-responsavel)).
- **Responsible-gambling access in Portuguese-BR** is prominent by design: **CVV 188**, and **Gambling Therapy** offers support in **Português (Brasil)** — see the block at the top of this page.

For deeper Brazil-specific live-results context, see [Live Tournament Data & Results](../Live_Tournament_Data_and_Results.md).
