# Regulatory Analysis Prompts

## Description
**Regulatory Analysis Prompts** are prompt engineering commands specifically designed to leverage Large Language Models (LLMs) in interpreting, summarizing, comparing, and assessing the impact of laws, regulations, policies, and compliance standards. They are crucial tools for professionals in the legal, compliance, and risk and governance (GRC) fields, enabling the rapid triage of large volumes of legal text and the identification of specific obligations and risks. The technique is based on providing the AI with the regulatory text (or a link to it) and requesting a structured and actionable analysis, transforming complex legal language into practical insights for decision-making and the implementation of internal policies. Its effectiveness lies in the AI's ability to process linguistic nuances and identify patterns and requirements faster than manual reading, especially in a landscape of constant regulatory change [1] [2].

## Examples
```
1. **New Law Impact Analysis:** "You are a Senior Compliance Analyst. Analyze the text of the 'General Data Protection Law (LGPD) - Law No. 13,709/2018' (text provided below). Identify the 5 main obligations that affect a medium-sized technology company in the healthcare sector. For each obligation, describe the risk of non-compliance and suggest a mitigation action in table format."
2. **Regulatory Comparison:** "Compare the European Union's 'General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)' with the 'California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)'. Focus specifically on the requirements for user consent and data access rights. Present the comparison in a table with the columns: Requirement, GDPR (Detail), CCPA (Detail), Key Difference."
3. **Extracting Policy Requirements:** "Based on the 'Information Security Policy Manual' (text provided), extract all mandatory training requirements for employees. For each requirement, generate a 100-word training prompt that can be used in an e-learning module."
4. **Identifying Compliance Gaps:** "Analyze the 'ISO 27001:2022 Standard' (text provided) and compare it with our 'Internal Security Audit Report' (text provided). Identify the 3 largest compliance gaps and classify them by risk level (High, Medium, Low). Suggest a 3-step action plan to close each gap."
5. **Actionable Executive Summary:** "Read the new EU 'Digital Markets Act (DMA)' (text provided). Write a 250-word executive summary for the Board of Directors. The summary should focus only on the strategic impact and the investment decisions the company should consider over the next 6 months."
6. **Creating an Audit Checklist:** "Based on 'Central Bank Regulation No. X' (text provided), create a 10-item audit checklist to ensure compliance. Each item should be a 'Yes/No' question and include the specific reference to the article or section of the regulation."
7. **Translating Legal Language:** "Translate Article 15 of the 'Civil Code' (text provided) into plain, accessible language that can be understood by a layperson client. Maintain legal accuracy but eliminate technical jargon."
```

## Best Practices
**1. Provide Detailed Context and Reference Documents:** Attach or paste the full text of the regulation, law, or policy. Specify the sector, jurisdiction, and objective of the analysis. **2. Define the Role and Output Format:** Begin the prompt by defining the AI's role (e.g., "You are a Senior Compliance Analyst"). Request the result in a structured format (table, list, JSON) to facilitate integration and review. **3. Use the Chain-of-Thought (CoT) Technique:** Ask the AI to first explain the reasoning behind its analysis before providing the final answer. This increases transparency and auditability. **4. Focus on Actions and Risks:** Instead of just asking for a summary, request concrete actions, risk mitigation plans, and the identification of compliance gaps. **5. Iteration and Human Validation:** Use the AI as a first-line assistant. The final result should always be reviewed and validated by a qualified legal or compliance professional.

## Use Cases
**1. Regulatory Compliance:** Identifying and monitoring compliance obligations in highly regulated sectors (financial, healthcare, energy). **2. Risk Analysis and Governance (GRC):** Rapidly assessing the impact of new laws or regulatory changes on corporate risk profiles and governance structure. **3. Legal Due Diligence:** Triaging and summarizing legal and regulatory documents during mergers, acquisitions, or audits. **4. Training and Internal Communication:** Creating simplified training materials and internal communications about new compliance policies for non-legal employees. **5. Policy Development:** Generating drafts of internal policies, standard operating procedures (SOPs), and audit checklists based on specific regulatory requirements. **6. Litigation and Legal Research:** Analyzing case law and precedents to identify relevant arguments and decision patterns in specific cases [1] [4].

## Pitfalls
**1. Hallucinations and Legal Inaccuracy:** The AI may generate factually incorrect information or mistaken legal interpretations (hallucinations). Overreliance without human validation is the greatest risk. **2. Bias and Lack of Context:** The AI may be influenced by its training data, resulting in biases or failing to consider the specific jurisdictional context, local case law, or cultural nuances. **3. Token Limits and Long Documents:** Extensive regulations may exceed the AI's token limit, forcing the document to be split and potentially losing the overall context or the interconnections between sections. **4. Data Security and Confidentiality:** Entering internal, confidential, or non-public regulatory documents into open or insecure AI models poses a serious risk of data leakage and breach of professional confidentiality. **5. Jargon and Ambiguity:** Poorly formulated prompts that use excessive jargon or are ambiguous will lead to generic or irrelevant results. Precision in the prompt's language is fundamental to the accuracy of the output [3].
