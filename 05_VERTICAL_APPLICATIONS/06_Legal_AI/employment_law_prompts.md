# Employment Law Prompts

## Description
Employment Law Prompts are specialized, structured instructions designed to be used with Large Language Models (LLMs) and other generative AI tools, with the goal of assisting Human Resources (HR) professionals, in-house lawyers, and legal consultants with matters related to labor legislation. These prompts are formulated to generate drafts of legal documents, analyze compliance risks, summarize case law, compare worker classifications (employee vs. independent contractor), and create internal policies, such as confidentiality agreements and paid time off policies. The effectiveness of these prompts lies in their ability to provide specific legal context (jurisdiction, type of law) and define the desired persona and output format, turning the AI into a highly efficient "legal intern" for front-line tasks.

## Examples
```
1.  **Termination Risk Analysis:**
    `You are a labor attorney with 10 years of experience. Analyze the following for-cause termination scenario [INSERT CASE DETAILS: performance history, warnings, reason for termination]. List the top 5 legal considerations and litigation risks under the law of the state of [STATE/JURISDICTION]. Present the response in a table format with columns for 'Risk', 'Legal Basis', and 'Recommended Action'.`
2.  **Worker Classification Comparison:**
    `Explain the main legal differences between an 'employee' and an 'independent contractor' under U.S. federal law (and the law of [STATE]). Create a 10-point checklist for HR to use in determining the correct classification, focusing on the criteria of control and independence. The tone should be informative and educational.`
3.  **Paid Time Off (PTO) Policy Draft:**
    `Draft a paid time off (PTO) policy for a U.S.-based remote workforce, considering the minimum PTO accrual and usage requirements in the states of California, New York, and Texas. Include clauses on the payment of unused PTO upon termination of the contract, as required by law. The format should be a draft internal policy document.`
4.  **Confidentiality Agreement Draft:**
    `Create a draft of a basic employee confidentiality agreement (NDA) for use in the U.S. The agreement should include non-disclosure clauses, return of company information upon termination of the contract, and post-termination obligations. The tone should be formal and legally precise.`
5.  **Hiring/Termination Compliance Guide:**
    `Generate a short compliance guide for the HR team when hiring and terminating employees in the state of [STATE/JURISDICTION]. The guide should cover the necessary legal documentation, wage and hour considerations, and practices to avoid discrimination. The format should be a numbered checklist.`
6.  **Remote Work Issues Analysis:**
    `Write a summary of the main legal issues to consider before implementing a hybrid or remote work policy across multiple states ([STATE 1], [STATE 2]). Focus on state taxes, workers' compensation laws, and time-tracking requirements for non-exempt employees. The format should be a concise internal memo.`
7.  **Termination Letter Language:**
    `Generate template language for an employment termination letter (without cause) that avoids creating implied promises or admissions of liability. Include a section on the finalization of benefits and eligibility for COBRA. The tone should be neutral and factual.`
```

## Best Practices
*   **Define the Persona and Audience:** Begin the prompt by defining the AI's role (e.g., "You are an experienced labor attorney") and the target audience of the output (e.g., "Write at a level that an HR manager can understand").
*   **Specify the Jurisdiction:** Employment Law is highly jurisdiction-dependent. Always include the specific state, country, or law (e.g., "under California law," "in compliance with the Brazilian CLT").
*   **Provide Context and Data:** Include as much relevant context as possible (e.g., "the employee received 3 formal warnings in the last 6 months") to avoid "hallucinations" and ensure legal accuracy.
*   **Require the Format:** Specify the output format (e.g., "table," "checklist," "email draft") to ensure the result is immediately useful.
*   **Human Validation (Mandatory):** **Never** use the AI's output as final legal advice or as a document without thorough review and validation by a qualified legal professional. The AI should be treated as a "first draft" or "intern."

## Use Cases
*   **Compliance and Auditing:** Create compliance checklists for hiring, termination, wages and hours, and leave laws (e.g., FMLA, state laws).
*   **Document Drafting:** Generate initial drafts of HR policies (e.g., code of conduct, remote work policy, PTO policy), confidentiality agreements (NDAs), and termination letters.
*   **Risk Analysis:** Assess the legal risks of personnel decisions, such as terminations, job reclassifications, or the implementation of new employee monitoring technologies.
*   **Training and Communication:** Create concise training materials for managers and employees on topics such as harassment, discrimination, and the ethical use of AI in the workplace.
*   **Legal Research:** Summarize the implications of new laws or court decisions (case law) for the company's internal policies.

## Pitfalls
*   **Legal Hallucinations:** The AI may generate false but convincing legal information (hallucinations), especially in less common jurisdictions or very recent laws. This can lead to serious compliance errors.
*   **Breach of Confidentiality and Secrecy:** Inputting confidential employee data or privileged information (such as details of a litigation case) into the prompt can violate professional secrecy and data privacy policies.
*   **Algorithmic Bias:** The AI may perpetuate or amplify existing biases in the training data, resulting in policies or analyses that lead to discriminatory outcomes (e.g., in resume screening processes or performance analysis).
*   **Overgeneralization:** The AI may fail to account for the nuances of state or local laws (e.g., differences in non-compete or salary transparency laws), especially in the U.S., where labor laws vary widely.
*   **Lack of Source:** Many generative AI tools do not provide citations or sources for their legal responses, making it difficult to verify the accuracy and timeliness of the information.
