# Copyright Analysis Prompts

## Description
**Copyright Analysis Prompts** are detailed instructions provided to Large Language Models (LLMs) to perform specific legal tasks related to copyright and intellectual property. The focus is on using AI as an assistant for drafting, research, summarizing, and analyzing legal documents, such as contracts, case law, and creative texts, in light of copyright legislation. Effectiveness depends on the precision of the prompt and human oversight to mitigate risks such as "hallucinations" and leakage of confidential information. This technique is fundamental for attorneys and creative professionals seeking to streamline their routine and ensure legal compliance in a constantly evolving digital environment [1] [2].

## Examples
```
**1. Infringement Risk Analysis:** "Act as an intellectual property attorney. Analyze the following code snippet [INSERT CODE] and compare it with the MIT license. Identify any risk of copyright infringement and suggest a rewrite to mitigate the risk. The result should be a numbered list." **2. Case Law Summary:** "Summarize the most recent STJ case law on 'fair use' of digital works. The summary should be in table format with the columns: Case, Main Decision, Implications for Copyright." **3. Clause Drafting:** "Draft a copyright clause for a graphic design services agreement, ensuring that all economic rights to the final work are fully transferred to the contracting party after payment. The clause should be clear and concise." **4. Terms of Use Analysis:** "Review the Terms of Use of a generative AI service (attached below) and highlight all sections that address the intellectual property of user-generated content. For each section, assess whether the term is favorable to the user or to the company. Use bold for terms unfavorable to the user." **5. Takedown Notice:** "Draft a formal *takedown* notice, on behalf of my client [CLIENT NAME], to the platform [PLATFORM NAME], demanding the immediate removal of the content [CONTENT DESCRIPTION AND URL] for copyright infringement of the work [PROTECTED WORK NAME]. Include the good-faith statement and the penalty for perjury." **6. Plagiarism and Similarity Analysis:** "Act as a text analysis expert. Compare 'Text A' [INSERT TEXT A] with 'Text B' [INSERT TEXT B]. Calculate the percentage of similarity and highlight the passages that constitute direct plagiarism. Issue an opinion indicating whether there are signs of copyright infringement, justifying it on the basis of originality and expression." **7. Image Rights Verification:** "Analyze the image [ATTACH IMAGE] and determine the type of usage license (e.g., Creative Commons, Royalty-Free, All Rights Reserved). If it is Creative Commons, specify the variation (e.g., CC BY-SA). If it is for commercial use, list the usage restrictions and required attribution."
```

## Best Practices
**1. Define a Clear Role:** Assign the AI a specific role (e.g., "Act as an IP attorney", "Act as a paralegal"). **2. Specificity and Context:** Provide detailed and unambiguous context, including the applicable legislation (e.g., "in light of the Brazilian Copyright Law, Law No. 9,610/98"). **3. Data Security (Crucial):** Remove confidential and sensitive information from the prompt before submitting it to the AI. **4. Output Format:** Be very specific about the desired output format (e.g., "Summarize in a table with 3 columns: Clause, Risk, Suggested Adjustment"). **5. Source Verification:** Request citations and verify sources to avoid "hallucinations" (invented facts). **6. Simple Tasks:** Break complex tasks into a series of simpler prompts. **7. Adjustment Suggestions:** Guide the AI to propose alternative wording for legal terms, increasing legal certainty.

## Use Cases
**Contract Review:** Identifying Intellectual Property (IP), confidentiality, and license clauses in contracts. **Legal Research:** Summarizing case law and legislation on specific copyright topics (e.g., fair use, AI-generated works). **Infringement Analysis:** Comparing a text, code, or image with protected material to identify possible violations. **Document Drafting:** Drafting IP clauses, *takedown* notices, or terms of use. **Risk Analysis:** Assessing IP risk in new projects or marketing campaigns, especially regarding the use of AI-generated content.

## Pitfalls
**Hallucinations:** The AI may invent nonexistent legal precedents or statutes. **Confidentiality:** Entering confidential data into the prompt can lead to information leakage, as the data may be used for model training. **Lack of Nuance:** The AI may have difficulty understanding contextual nuances of the law, always requiring human oversight and validation by a qualified professional. **Training Bias:** The model may reflect biases present in the training data, affecting the impartiality of the analysis.
