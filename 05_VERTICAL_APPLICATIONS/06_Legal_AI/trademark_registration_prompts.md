# Trademark Registration Prompts

## Description
Trademark Registration Prompts are specialized instructions provided to Large Language Models (LLMs), such as ChatGPT, Gemini, or Claude, to assist in various stages of the intellectual property protection process, with a focus on trademarks. They are designed to increase the efficiency and accuracy of tasks typically performed by attorneys, paralegals, and entrepreneurs, such as drafting descriptions of goods and services, Nice classification, generating prior art search strategies, and managing renewal deadlines. Applying AI in this field transforms IP management from a reactive function into a proactive and structured strategy.

## Examples
```
1. **Description of Goods and Services (Nice Classification):**
```
Act as an experienced trademark paralegal. I need a detailed and acceptable description for the INPI/USPTO for the trademark "TechFlow". The product is a cloud-based workflow management software for small and medium-sized businesses. Include the most likely Nice class and the justification.
```

2. **Prior Art Search Strategy:**
```
Generate a comprehensive prior art search strategy for the trademark name "AuraBloom" in the organic cosmetics sector (Class 3). The strategy should include searching for identical and similar marks, phonetic and visual, in national and international databases, and suggest additional key search terms.
```

3. **Refusal Risk Analysis (Technical Opinion):**
```
Analyze the following trademark name: "Café Supremo". The product is roasted and ground coffee (Class 30). Based on the principles of distinctiveness and common refusals, provide a technical opinion on the risk of refusal, citing examples of weak or descriptive marks.
```

4. **Defining SMART Goals for IP (New Product):**
```
Create a SMART goal (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) for the IP protection of our new fitness app, "FitPulse", which will launch in 90 days. The goal should focus on trademark registration and source code protection.
```

5. **Drafting an IP Contract Clause:**
```
Draft an intellectual property clause for a service agreement with a freelance developer. The clause must ensure that all IP rights to the developed software are fully assigned to my company, including the right to trademark and patent registration.
```

6. **Monitoring Renewal Deadlines:**
```
List all IP assets (Trademark X, Patent Y) expiring within the next 18 months and generate a timeline of the legal steps required for renewal, including the deadline for the maintenance fee.
```

7. **Creating a Registrable Trademark Name (Creative Search):**
```
Create 5 strong, original trademark name options with a high probability of registrability at the INPI/USPTO for the following segment: Plant-based energy drinks. The names should be suggestive but not descriptive.
```
```

## Best Practices
- **Legal Contextualization:** Always include the **country/jurisdiction** (e.g., INPI - Brazil, USPTO - USA) and the relevant **Nice class** to refine the AI's response.
- **Mandatory Human Review:** Never submit an AI-generated legal document without the **final review and validation by a qualified professional** (attorney or paralegal).
- **Extreme Specificity:** The more details about the product, service, and target market, the more accurate the description suggestion or risk analysis will be.
- **Use of SMART Goals:** Use prompts to turn vague IP concerns into **clear, time-bound actions** (e.g., "Generate a SMART goal for trademark protection before the product launch").

## Use Cases
- **Drafting Petitions:** Generating drafts of registration application sections, such as the description of goods and services.
- **Preliminary Feasibility Analysis:** Initial assessment of the distinctiveness and collision risk of a proposed trademark name.
- **IP Portfolio Management:** Creating tracking systems and reminders for registration renewal and maintenance deadlines.
- **Internal Training and Compliance:** Developing training materials for employees on IP policies and preventing accidental disclosure.
- **Naming Strategy:** Generating creative trademark names that maximize the likelihood of registration.

## Pitfalls
- **Overreliance:** AI does not replace legal judgment. The risk of **hallucinations** (false or incorrect information) is high in a specific legal field.
- **Breach of Confidentiality:** Entering confidential information or trade secrets into public AI prompts can violate confidentiality agreements.
- **Overgeneralizing the Law:** AI may not be up to date with the specific **nuances and precedents** of each jurisdiction (e.g., recent INPI or USPTO decisions).
- **Vague Descriptions:** Poorly crafted prompts result in descriptions of goods and services that are too broad or too narrow, which can compromise trademark protection.
