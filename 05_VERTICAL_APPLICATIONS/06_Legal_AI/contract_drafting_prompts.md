# Contract Drafting Prompts

## Description
A contract drafting prompt is a specific and detailed instruction provided to a large language model (LLM) to generate, review, or analyze a legal document with legal precision. Unlike a generic prompt, the legal prompt must include **legal and jurisdictional context**, specify **mandatory clauses**, and indicate the desired **tone and format**. Mastering this technique is crucial for legal professionals seeking to optimize drafting time and ensure legal compliance, using AI as an acceleration tool, but always under the final supervision of an attorney.

## Examples
```
1.  **Service Agreement:** "Draft a software development services agreement in Brazil, including confidentiality clauses, delivery deadlines, and intellectual property rights. Use a formal tone and a format with numbered clauses."
2.  **International Employment Contract:** "Write an employment contract for a remote employee in Spain who reports to a company headquartered in the United States. Include clauses on salary, working hours, benefits, and dispute resolution under Spanish jurisdiction."
3.  **NDA with Data Clause:** "Generate a non-disclosure agreement (NDA) between two retail companies in Brazil, with a personal data protection clause in accordance with the General Data Protection Law (LGPD). Use formal language and divide the document into sections."
4.  **Real Estate Purchase and Sale Agreement:** "Act as a Brazilian real estate attorney. Draft the terms of a promise of purchase and sale agreement for a residential property located in São Paulo, Brazil. The prompt should include: 1) Full identification of the parties (seller and buyer); 2) Total price and payment terms (down payment and installments); 3) Rescission clause and penalty; 4) Deadline for handover of keys and possession. Use the *Layering* technique to structure the document."
5.  **Terms of Service (SaaS Software):** "Create Terms of Service (ToS) for a B2B SaaS (Software as a Service) product. The document must be governed by the law of the state of Delaware, USA. Include sections on: 1) License to use; 2) Limitation of liability; 3) Termination-for-breach policy; 4) Choice of forum for dispute resolution. The tone should be clear and concise."
6.  **Contract Amendment:** "Based on the attached service agreement (instruction), draft a contract amendment to extend the term for an additional 12 months and adjust the monthly amount by 15% (IGPM index). The amendment should be concise and make clear reference to the original clauses being modified. Jurisdiction: Brazil."
7.  **Non-Compete Clause (CNO):** "Generate a robust non-compete clause (CNO) for the employment contract of a senior executive. The clause must specify: 1) Duration of 24 months after the end of the contract; 2) Geographic area of restriction (Latin America); 3) Financial compensation (monthly amount during the restriction); 4) Penalty for breach. Ask the AI to justify the reasonableness of the clause based on Brazilian case law."
```

## Best Practices
1.  **Defining Role and Context:** Ask the AI to act as an expert (e.g., "attorney specializing in labor law") and define the type of contract and industry.
2.  **Jurisdictional Specification:** Always include the applicable jurisdiction and legislation (e.g., "under the Federal Labor Law of Brazil").
3.  **Inclusion of Essential Clauses:** List the mandatory clauses (Subject, Deadlines, Confidentiality, Termination, etc.).
4.  **Detailed Format and Tone:** Explicitly request the format (numbered clauses, sections) and the tone (formal, clear).
5.  **Use of Advanced Techniques:** Use *Few-Shot* (with example clauses), *Chain-of-Thought* (breaking into logical steps), or *Layering* (modular construction) for complex contracts.
6.  **Mandatory Human Review:** AI accelerates the work, but final validation by an attorney is non-negotiable to ensure legal accuracy.

## Use Cases
1.  **Initial Contract Drafting:** Generating drafts of standard contracts (Service Agreements, Purchase and Sale, Leases).
2.  **Jurisdictional Adaptation:** Creating contracts for different countries, specifying the local legislation and applicable rules.
3.  **Inclusion of Specific Clauses:** Generating complex clauses such as NDAs (Non-Disclosure Agreements), IPR (Intellectual Property Rights), or data protection clauses (LGPD, GDPR).
4.  **Remote Employment Contracts:** Drafting international employment contracts, taking into account different jurisdictions and work arrangements.
5.  **Review and Analysis:** Generating prompts to analyze existing contracts, identify risks, summarize terms, and compare against legal *benchmarks*.

## Pitfalls
1.  **Jurisdictional Ambiguity:** Failing to specify the country and the concrete rule, resulting in a legally invalid or unenforceable contract.
2.  **Lack of Specificity:** Vague prompts that result in generic and unusable contracts, requiring extensive manual review.
3.  **Inclusion of Sensitive Data:** Entering personal or confidential information directly into the prompt, compromising security and privacy.
4.  **Omission of Human Review:** Blindly trusting the AI's result without final validation by a legal professional, which is unacceptable in legal documents.
5.  **Using a Single Prompt for Multiple Jurisdictions:** Using the same prompt for different countries, ignoring the need for legal and regulatory adaptation.
