# Poetry Prompts

## Description
The **Poetry Prompts** technique is a specialized application of Prompt Engineering focused on generating high-quality, structured poetic content with Large Language Models (LLMs). Its main goal is to transcend the production of generic, superficial verses, guiding the AI to create poems that demonstrate depth, complexity, authorial voice, and adherence to specific poetic forms.

The core principle of this technique is **specificity**. Instead of a vague prompt like "Write a poem about love", the user should provide a detailed set of constraints and creative elements. This includes defining the **Theme** (e.g., the fleeting nature of love in the digital age), the **Tone** (e.g., melancholic and ironic), the **Structure** (e.g., a Shakespearean sonnet with an ABAB CDCD EFEF GG rhyme scheme), the **Imagery** (e.g., metaphors of broken glass and screen light), and the **Perspective** (e.g., the view of a distant observer).

Beyond creation, Poetry Prompts are crucial in the phases of **ideation** (overcoming creative block), **writing** (continuing verses or finding rhymes), and **editing/evaluation** (requesting specific critiques on meter, flow, and language). The best practice is to treat the AI as a co-author or a creative "compass", using its suggestions selectively and retaining final judgment over the work.

## Examples
```
1. **Ideation and Structure:**
`"Act as a Brazilian modernist poet. Write a 12-line free-verse poem on the theme 'Urban loneliness seen through a window on a rainy day'. The tone should be one of contemplation and disillusionment. Include the image of a rusty streetcar and the smell of strong coffee."`

2. **Generation with Formal Constraints:**
`"Compose a Haiku (5-7-5 syllables) in Portuguese. The theme is 'The first ray of sunlight'. The main image should be dew evaporating on a leaf. The tone should be one of quiet hope."`

3. **Continuation and Specific Rhyme:**
`"Continue the following quatrain in a ballad style, ensuring that the third line rhymes with 'mar' and the fourth line rhymes with 'lugar'. The theme is the search for a lost treasure.
'O mapa antigo, de couro e pó,
Mostrava a ilha, longe daqui,
Onde o vento sopra, sem dó,
E o tempo parou, sem ti.'"`

4. **Use of Imagery and Metaphors:**
`"Write a 150-word prose poem. The theme is anxiety. Use the metaphor of a broken clock and the image of a bird trapped in a cage of ribs. The tone should be one of suffocating urgency."`

5. **Critique and Editing (Flow Improvement):**
`"Analyze the following line: 'A luz que brilha muito forte, ela me faz chorar'. Suggest 5 alternatives to rewrite it, improving the rhythm and meter while preserving the sense of intensity and pain. The poem is a sonnet."`

6. **Change of Perspective:**
`"Rewrite the attached poem (paste the poem) from the perspective of an inanimate object present in the scene, such as a mirror or a statue. Keep the original theme, but shift the tone to one of quiet skepticism."`

7. **Specific Author Style:**
`"Write a poem of 4 stanzas, each with 4 lines (quatrain), in the style of Fernando Pessoa (orthonym). The theme is the inevitability of destiny. The tone should be philosophical and resigned. Use formal vocabulary."`
```

## Best Practices
1. **Extreme Specificity:** Always define the Theme, Tone, Structure (e.g., sonnet, haiku), Imagery, and Perspective. Vague prompts result in generic poetry.
2. **Selective Use:** Treat the AI as a co-author or editing assistant, not as the primary author. Use it for ideation, rhyme, and critique, but preserve the authorial voice.
3. **Iteration and Critique:** Ask the AI to critique specific aspects (meter, flow, language) and use the feedback to refine the draft.
4. **Multiple Suggestions:** Ask the AI for several options (e.g., "Give 5 rhyme suggestions for the word 'coração'") to have more creative material.

## Use Cases
1. **Ideation:** Overcome creative block by suggesting imagery, themes, styles, or references to famous poems.
2. **Writing:** Continue a poem, find rhyming words, suggest the use of specific imagery/language.
3. **Editing/Evaluation:** Critique and refine the draft, correct lines for better flow/rhythm, adjust the tone.
4. **Learning:** Better understand poetic structures and writing techniques, such as meter and fixed forms (sonnet, haiku).

## Pitfalls
1. **Vague Prompts:** Result in superficial, generic poems lacking authorial voice.
2. **Over-Reliance:** Relying on the AI for every step, which can lead to a loss of originality and personal voice.
3. **Accepting the AI's Critique as Absolute Truth:** The AI may miss subtle poetic nuances; the author must have the final judgment.
4. **Ignoring Structure:** Not specifying the poetic form (sonnet, haiku, etc.) leads to unstructured results.
